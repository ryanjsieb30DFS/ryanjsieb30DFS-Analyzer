# Coors Field (Colorado Rockies — Denver, CO)

**Created 2026-06-10 by the post-autopsy review for MLB Classic 6.9.26** — no park
file existed pre-lock (pre-flight stub step was skipped that slate). Physical
profile below is built from that slate's articles (The Stone 6/9/26) plus general
knowledge; treat as a starting point until more slates accumulate.

## Physical profile

- Altitude ~5,200 ft — thin air suppresses pitch break and carries fly balls;
  the league's most extreme run environment.
- Run environment inflates BOTH sides of every game, not just the favorite's.
- Large outfield (compensating for carry) also inflates BABIP/extra-base hits,
  so non-HR hitters (gap/speed types) get a boost too, not only power bats.

## Strategic notes (how stacks play here)

- The field reflexively concentrates ownership on the Coors *favorite/visitor*
  side when it's the slate's highest implied team total; the home (COL) side
  routinely goes overlooked at a fraction of the ownership despite sharing the
  same run environment.
- Cheap COL bats double as one-off salary relief that stays correlated to the
  slate's best game environment.

## Per-slate observations

- **2026-06-10 (MLB Classic 6.9.26, CHC @ COL, 6.80/5.95 implied):** The
  Coors favorite (CHC) drew ~90% combined stack ownership and underpaid it; the
  low-owned COL side carried winning lineups instead — Hunter Goodman 22.0 pts
  at 8.6% own (slate-defining play, $5k), Willi Castro 11.0 at 4.3%, Ezequiel
  Tovar in three of the $5k top-10, Kyle Karros in the $5k winner. Proved: Coors
  leverage lives on the unowned side of the game, not in the consensus stack —
  a COL mini-stack/one-off was near-free equity while the CHC mega-stack was an
  ownership tax. (User lineup's lone Coors piece, Castro at $4,200, was the
  right idea and a winning-lineup staple.)
