# MMA — Player pool
_Fades already removed (only Shara Magomedov excluded); this is a ranked hand-build reference of the 25 rosterable fighters, best→worst by GPP play-priority — individual plays only, never a lineup. Ownership cited as the articles state it (Stone/SIN DK Own.% column / DailyFan), the projected own is only a reference._

1. **Abdul Rakhman Yakhyaev** ($9,500, own 49/36 SIN/DF) — *Core* — The slate's premier smash-anchor: biggest favorite (-600), best ITD/round-1 prop on the card (-325 / -130), top projection (96.58 SIN / 88.17 DF), 7 R1 finishes in 9 wins. Unlike most top chalk he carries a floor (G&P/sub/decision paths), so he's not KO-or-bust; risk is he's the chalkiest play on the board (SIN ~half the field) — don't ride him identically in both bullets.

2. **Ikram Aliskerov** ($9,100, own 42/28 SIN/DF) — *Core* — Wrestler-finisher whom Tim calls "the most likely outcome" — take Ferreira down and finish on the mat (-310, -160 ITD, four of five UFC fights ended R1). Wins have scored 109/120/110/108/98, so many paths to a smash; risk is the vendor own split (lean the lower DF 28%) and Ferreira's live one-punch KO equity if they stand.

3. **Javier Reyes** ($8,500, own 21/29 SIN/DF) — *Core* — Best value anchor and a finisher, not trimmable secondary chalk: elite ITD/R1 prop for the price (+130 / +350), 19 of 23 wins finished, and Reyes dictates where it goes against a durability-shy Ofli who lands 0% of his takedowns. Brett "want[s] to be overweight"; the cap is he's not a clean minute-winner if the finish never comes.

4. **Daniil Donchenko** ($9,400, own 28/28 SIN/DF) — *Core* — The Anchor-Equivalence alternative to Yakhyaev: same -400-class finish fight ($100 cheaper, -460 / -260 ITD) into a disastrous spot for the gas-tank-and-defensive-wrestling-deficient Berggren. Uncontested if the cheaper finisher hits; risk is he needs an R1 KO (only +165) to be truly optimal, so a 91-type score that misses is live.

5. **Abus Magomedov** ($8,300, own 22/26 SIN/DF) — *Pivot* — Highest-ceiling mid-priced piece and a named PLAY: the tied-3rd-likeliest finish on the card (-400), +110 ITD with a clean mat-finish path (Olek has been subbed in 4 of his last 5 losses), 100-pt upside. Risk is his own cardio/durability if Olek survives early and turns it into a grind.

6. **Michal Oleksiejczuk** ($7,900, own 31/34 SIN/DF) — *Pivot* — The other side of the same -400 banger and the field's slight lean: 108 DK/win over 10 wins (mostly early KOs), +175 ITD, never knocked down/out in the UFC. Real R1–2 KO ceiling; cap is he won't wrestle and can drift into a lower-scoring decision if he can't get inside on Abus.

7. **Manuel Torres** ($8,000, own 45/45 SIN/DF) — *Pivot* — Top win-score-per-dollar on the card: every UFC win is an R1 finish, 107.7 DK/win, +110 ITD, "if he wins he'll score 100." Genuine ceiling, but it's a true pick'em (51.7%) and the combined main-event pile can hit 80–90% owned — the chalk-correct, undifferentiated side.

8. **Nazim Sadykhov** ($9,000, own 17/18 SIN/DF) — *Pivot* — The best low-owned ceiling-with-substance play: an upper-tier finisher the field sleeps on while it piles into the main event, ITD prop steaming to -110, 12 of 13 fights ITD, scored 122 in his last win despite being dropped. Risk is the chaotic profile — a comeback win that doesn't smash, or he eats damage and loses (62% win).

9. **Farman Hasanov** ($8,600, own 16/26 SIN/DF) — *Pivot* — ATT-debut fighter with an elite top game (-215 to finish, +115 ITD) whose R1–2 G&P TKOs hit 100; the SIN/DF ownership gap is the live edge — if SIN's 16% is right he's quiet leverage. Boom/bust: needs early top control, the line is drifting (-210 → -159), so treat as a fade-down and lean the lower own.

10. **Asu Almabayev** ($8,900, own 27/28 SIN/DF) — *Pivot* — The floor alternative in the mid-anchor group: tenacious wrestling-control (4.46 TD/15), and you want this one to reach the cards for control-time points (scored 97/70/104/118 in wins). It's the most-likely fight to go the distance (-200) and Johnson can keep it standing, so the ceiling is the capped piece, not the floor.

11. **Rafael Fiziev** ($8,200, own 42/37 SIN/DF) — *Pivot* — The underowned, more-technical half of the main event with five rounds and late-takedown upside; SIN's breakdown slightly prefers him. The leverage side of the most-owned fight on the card; risk is the coin flip (48.3%) plus a real median-score cap — he's a low-volume scorer (82/86/71/71 in past wins), and DF projects him lowest (57.97).

12. **Jean Matsumoto** ($8,400, own 18/16 SIN/DF) — *Pivot* — The rare low-own piece that satisfies the leverage rule *with* a floor: wrestling volume (5.37 sspm, 3 TD/15; scored 80 & 84 even in losses) and a buried 100-pt path when he wrestles with urgency (7 TD vs Basharat). Cap is the weird baseline — it's a distance-leaning fight and his decision wins only scored ~70.

13. **Tahir Abdullayev** ($8,300, own 21/23 SIN/DF) — *Dart* — The live-dog-with-a-floor differentiator: +105 hometown wrestler who finished 17 of 19 wins and can grind top time on the takedown-prone Nascimento, low-owned at a more-expensive-dog price. Wins on a grappling-led upset that still has a non-ceiling floor; risk is the ugly, clinch-heavy decision both styles point toward.

14. **Matheus Camilo** ($7,200, own 17/21 SIN/DF) — *Dart* — The steadier cheap option for the small field: odds value taking action (+157, +275 ITD), wrestles, and his chaotic opponent leaves him live early (Tim's "live dog" in the first half). Modest ceiling and ~38% win equity with a live finisher across the cage — a 490 salary-saver more than a swing.

15. **Julius Walker** ($6,700, own 7/5 SIN/DF) — *Dart* — The only true sub-5% piece and the slate's max-leverage swing: a +425 (~18%) dog whose shock KO of the card's best finisher would single-handedly win a tournament (the 6/20 live-dog lesson). Pure KO-or-bust — reserve for the 1,189 field, never force it into the 490 where binary darts get punished.

16. **Nursulton Ruziboev** ($8,800, own 22/20 SIN/DF) — *Dart* — Boom-or-bust finisher (92% career finish rate) priced just low enough to tempt, but Gordo flags him a "HORRIBLE minute winner" at 13.9 DK/5min — even his finishes only scored 100/104/97, and a decision win (68) is a dead roster. Needs the early ITD finish or the salary is wasted.

17. **Brunno Ferreira** ($7,100, own 21/26 SIN/DF) — *Dart* — Real one-punch power (+260 / +285 ITD, 14 of 15 wins finished) with a 100-pt ceiling if he clips Aliskerov early. Low leverage despite being a dog — he's the "most popular underdog," needs a finish to win (~28%), and his leaky takedown defense points to getting put on his back. Large-field sprinkle.

18. **Bekzat Almakhan** ($7,800, own 16/18 SIN/DF) — *Dart* — Live for the big shot anytime (10 of 12 wins by KO, +140 / +300 ITD); single-shot power keeps him dangerous all 15 vs a hittable Matsumoto. Cap: he's reliant on a finish in a fight that leans toward a decision he'd likely lose on output — a cheap binary, not a 490 piece.

19. **Jefferson Nascimento** ($7,900, own 22/28 SIN/DF) — *Dart* — Undefeated value win-equity play (52.2% DF) with late-arriving cardio and some boxing pop. Ceiling is the issue — it's the third-most-likely fight to reach a decision, a clinch-heavy low-scoring win is modal, and he's the field's side of a fight the strategy leverages against.

20. **Charles Johnson** ($7,300, own 20/20 SIN/DF) — *Dart* — Pure win-equity dart: the better striker with a clear path if he stays vertical, and "70 might be enough at 7300." But it's the most-likely fight to go the distance, his win-scores are 72/72/69, and Almabayev's wrestling can pin him — low ceiling, ~28% win.

21. **Michel Pereira** ($7,000, own 14/14 SIN/DF) — *Dart* — Cheap name-brand sprinkle with residual KO and wrestling equity, but in bad form (lost 3 of 4), a clear dog (~24%), and the eye test has the faded Shara as the more effective striker. Strictly a large-field salary-relief flier on a narrow upset.

22. **Eric Nolan** ($7,600, own 15/17 SIN/DF) — *Dart* — Sub-15% tough-guy dog (~39%) live on the feet if the debuting Hasanov gasses. Wins by out-working a tiring favorite to a finish or pace-driven decision; risk is there's no guaranteed offense — even a win can be a 20-strike round — and it needs the fade to actually happen.

23. **Andrey Pulyaev** ($7,400, own 16/16 SIN/DF) — *Dart* — Point-fighting dog who's the better minute-winner if this becomes a boring distance kickboxing affair (+180, +350 ITD, scored 90s in wins). But a decision win caps him around 50 DK — almost no smash equity, leverage only, min-salary flier.

24. **Kaan Ofli** ($7,700, own 18/18 SIN/DF) — *Dart* — One-dimensional grappler (0% TD accuracy, 0% TDD, poor durability) priced up only because Reyes draws action — Brett flatly "don't think he wins." Overpriced for the spot with a thin shock-decision/finish path; he's the side the slate is actively fading.

25. **Theodor Berggren** ($6,800, own 11/7 SIN/DF) — *Dart* — The lowest-owned fighter on the slate and a pure punt: +360 / +550-ITD newcomer in a disastrous stylistic matchup (bad cardio, exploitable defensive wrestling vs a high-pace meathead). Only viable as extreme salary relief in the largest field on a shock early KO; fails the small-field blended-EV floor.
