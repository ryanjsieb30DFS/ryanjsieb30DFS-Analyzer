## How to approach the slate
Finishing equity is everywhere (10 of 13 expected to finish), so the winning SE is a stack of **winners who finish**, anchored by a top grappler-finisher with downstream differentiation. You do **not** need the main event — there's plenty of finish ceiling elsewhere and the Fiziev/Torres pick'em could push 80–90% combined ownership.

Shape: one elite finisher anchor (Yakhyaev OR Donchenko/Aliskerov) → two or three finish-capable mid favorites in clean spots (Reyes, the Abus/Olek banger, Aliskerov) → one genuine leverage piece → a cheap slot that fits the field. Lean **chalk-with-one-swing in the 490** (small fields punish pure variance) and **more differentiated in the 1,189** (carry a sub-10% live dog). Sharp envelope: elite anchor, ≥1 sub-10% leverage piece, the two SEs built on **different conviction anchors**, judged on ceiling not floor.

## Decisions
1. **ANCHOR-EQUIVALENCE (mandatory) — MIX Yakhyaev and Donchenko/Aliskerov across the two SEs.** Yakhyaev (SIN 49/DF 36) and the cheaper top finishers Donchenko (28/28) / Aliskerov (42/28) are substitutable chalk-tier anchors — anchor **one** SE on Yakhyaev and the **other** on Donchenko (or Aliskerov), capturing uncontested leverage if the cheaper finisher hits and satisfying the never-share-identical-cores SE rule in one move. Asymmetric weighting does **not** apply — Yakhyaev has a floor, so don't under-weight him below default presence.
2. **PLAY the Abus/Oleksiejczuk banger on one side.** Mechanism: highest-ceiling mid-priced fight (tied 3rd-likeliest finish, 100-pt upside both ways) — either side is a live, finish-equity core.
3. **MIX Reyes as a value-tier core.** Mechanism: finish-capable favorite with many paths and +130 ITD — exempt from "trim secondary chalk"; his ownership (21/29) is earned by a clean finish profile, not a leak.
4. **PASS / structural-fade Shara at $9.2k.** Mechanism: a -400 favorite whose ceiling is capped at ~decision points without a rare quick KO — the fade bets on his modal outcome and is structural, not a narrative scouting guess.
5. **Field-split the leverage/cheap slot.** Mechanism: small 490 SE → a decision-floor/value-favorite cheap piece (e.g., Camilo); medium 1,189 SE → carry one sub-10% live dog (Walker) — binary punts are punished in small fields and rewarded in larger ones (validated 6/20). React only to **confirmed** news at lock; never swap a reliable scorer onto a KO-or-bust replacement on speculation.
