## Edges & tensions

- **Ownership runs opposite to win probability this slate.** The four most likely winners on the board — Coria (88% to win, 22% owned), Barbosa (83%, 24%), Kline (80%, 21%), Duncan (76%, 28%) — are all owned HALF of what the main event draws (Du Plessis 45%, per the DailyFan sheet). The field is paying for the marquee fight, not for win equity; the article explains why (each big favorite has a ceiling question), but the gap itself is the slate's structure.
- **Du Plessis is the board's biggest ownership-vs-ceiling mispricing.** He is 45% owned, yet the Matchups article's most likely outcome is him "winning via volume and optics and scoring 90-100 points" — he scored just 94 in a 147-strike decision over Strickland, and he's only +145 to finish inside the distance. Nearly half the field is anchored to a score that probably doesn't win a slate whose recent winners rostered six 96–137 point fighters.
- **Anchor-Equivalence: Du Plessis (45% own, $9.0K) and McMillen (42%, $8.2K) are near-identical chalk anchors to the field** — similar ownership, similar "very likely wins, ceiling not guaranteed" profiles (DDP's modal outcome is a 90–100 volume decision; McMillen has "already scored poorly in an extended brawl"). One tier down, Hooper 35% / Bashi 36% / Usman 37% form a second interchangeable cluster. Substitutable anchors at equal ownership mean the field has already mispriced the coin flip between them.
- **The chalk pairs are a duplication trap in these small fields.** The field will pair Du Plessis + McMillen in roughly 18.9% of lineups (~225 of the 1,189-field), Du Plessis + Usman — the main-event opponent stack — in ~16.7% (~199), Du Plessis + Bashi ~16.2%, Du Plessis + Hooper ~15.8%, McMillen + Usman ~15.5%, McMillen + Bashi ~15.1% (bundle chalk-combo estimates, and real fields correlate their chalk beyond these floors). A lineup carrying two of the top chalk starts the night sharing its bet with ~200 others.
- **Your own SE field reliably crowds the top-owned favorites and pairs the two chalkiest anchors.** In both of your 2 logged SE contests the field piled into Paddy Pimblett, Benoit Saint Denis, Damian Pinas, King Green, Terrance McKinney, Ryan Gandra, Max Holloway and Gable Steveson (each in 2 of 2), paired Max Holloway + Terrance McKinney / Max Holloway + Paddy Pimblett / King Green + Max Holloway (2 of 2), and the recurring fish-traps were Zach Reese, Benoit Saint Denis, Conor McGregor, Terrance McKinney, Cory Sandhagen, Nikita Krylov and Kai Kamaka III (2 of 2). None of those names fight tonight, but the pattern maps directly: tonight's analogue of the crowded Holloway-anchor-plus-partner stack is Du Plessis + McMillen — leverage-away lives outside that pairing.
- **Only two true sub-10% fighters exist on this 24-man board — Nicoll (5%) and Melisano (8%) — and both carry real if-they-win ceilings** (96.9 and 85.8 points, DailyFan). The article waves Nicoll off ("with a smaller portfolio I'd probably just fade him") while the same article concedes "in a win I would bet he's optimal" — that article-vs-itself gap, on the only genuine leverage on the board, is exactly the low-owned-definer profile that has decided your last two slates (Ofli 6/27, Bautista 7/12). Your leverage capture has fallen 100% → 60% → 0% over the last three slates, which makes this screen the slate's priority.
- **The articles and the sheet disagree on Franco's ownership.** Brett writes Franco "might be low owned too" and ranks him the #1 bottom-tier play (grappling upside, price, odds); the DailyFan sheet projects him at a very ordinary 24%. If the article is right and the sheet is high, Franco plays like leverage at secondary-chalk price; if the sheet is right, he's just another mid-owned coin flip. That uncertainty is itself signal.

## Field vs Sharp — how this slate gets played

**How the FIELD will play it.** The typical entry anchors the main event — Du Plessis at 45% (with a meaningful slice stacking Usman at 37% on top, ~199 lineups' worth in the Clinch) — then fills from the dense $7.9–8.2K mid-tier the articles hype: McMillen 42%, Bashi 36%, Hooper 35%, Ko 30%. That's why every top chalk combo above involves DDP or McMillen. Your own contest history says the same thing: in 2 of 2 logged SEs the field crowded the highest-owned favorites and paired the two chalkiest anchors together. The value shelf is thin, so the field's salary relief concentrates into the same few names (Ramirez 15%, Ricci 13%, Cannonier 15%) rather than the true punts.

**How a SHARP will play it.** MMA sharps run chalk-ownership builds — on your shared slates they averaged ~30% ownership per slot, essentially matching the field — but they differ in two specific ways (sharp playbook): they carry a sub-5% piece in ~15% of lineups where you have carried one in 0%, and they pick *which* favorites via ceiling, not marquee. On this slate that means the sharp's anchor comes from the highest points-if-win tier — Hooper (110 DK points per win average, highs of 134/142, -145 to finish) or Barbosa (108/109 in her two first-round subs) — not automatically from the 45%-owned main eventer whose modal score is 90–100. Their leverage piece is Nicoll at 5% or Melisano at 8%, the board's only genuine darts, or the cheap live-dog tier (Ramirez 15%). And your last two MMA winners show the winning sharp shape precisely: ~40% average ownership, zero dead slots, six fighters who all won, with exactly ONE low-owned converter (Bautista at 15%) doing the separating.

**The gap.** The field pays 45% for Du Plessis's 90–100-point volume decision and shares him with McMillen in ~1 of 5 lineups; the sharp gets a bigger if-win score at 22–35% ownership (Hooper/Barbosa/Montes tier) and spends the saved ownership on the one converter the field skips. The divergence is not chalk-vs-punt — it's *which* chalk, plus whether the sixth slot is a 13–15% live winner or a shared salary-relief piece.

**How a sharp uses the key players:**

- **Du Plessis ($9.0K, 45%)** — The sharp is underweight, not off: the article itself lands at "coming in a bit underweight on the whole to this fight." Anchoring him means 45% of the field shares your anchor while his most likely score is 90–100, so the build's uniqueness must come entirely from the other five slots — and at $9K he leaves $41K for five slots ($8.2K average), which funnels straight into the exact mid-tier the field jams. What a sharp refuses around him: McMillen (the ~18.9% pair) and the Usman opponent-stack (~16.7% — in an SE the stack caps your own ceiling because one of them must lose points to the other).
- **McMillen ($8.2K, 42%)** — A finish-capable favorite in a clean matchup, which your codified framework rule says is NOT trimmable chalk on ownership alone: 130-point debut, 100+ strike pace, +120 ITD. The sharp holds him near the field's number but refuses the DDP pairing — carrying McMillen *without* DDP keeps the piece while dodging the ~225-lineup crowd.
- **Usman ($7.2K, 37%)** — His 37% is cash-game-flavored ownership (the article calls him a better cash play). The sharp's angle is narrower: he averages 120 per win, and the article flags a live "optimal even in a loss" path (he's scored 98 and 69 in five-round losses; DDP has allowed 71/45/51 in five-round wins). A sharp either carries that specific thesis or goes under the field — never the DDP+Usman stack.
- **Hooper ($9.2K, 35%)** — The article's #1 tournament play on ceiling ("capability to break the slate"). Ownership is discounted by two straight RD1 KO losses — fear, not math, since he's -340 vs an 0-3 opponent. This is the sharp's anchor-tier overweight; the construction cost is that $9.2K plus a thin value shelf forces at least one sub-$7K piece, which is where Nicoll/Melisano/Ricci enter.
- **Bashi ($7.9K, 36%)** — Grappling scorer who "absolutely crushed" in wins (110 real, 144 on DWCS) against a Delgado who conceded 4 takedowns to Fili. Finish-capable favorite → hold near field per the framework exemption; the sharp just declines to pair him with BOTH other mid-tier chalks (McMillen+Bashi is a ~15.1% combo).
- **Ko ($8.8K, 30%)** — The article's own game-theory read is "coming in lighter on Ko is probably correct" — he's a regression candidate (105/101 decisions but 83 on DWCS, only +235 ITD, step-up in competition). The sharp runs him under the field and treats him as the direct pivot casualty of preferring Hooper/Barbosa at the same salary band.
- **Coria ($9.8K, 22%)** — Only 4 of the last 14 fighters priced $9.8K were optimal (article), his 100–110 modal score needs to be 130 to matter, and pairing him with DDP eats $18.8K in two slots — forcing two picks from the three-fighter sub-$7K shelf. A sharp who wants the 88% win equity accepts that construction tax knowingly or passes.
- **Nicoll ($6.4K, 5%) / Melisano ($6.8K, 8%)** — The board's only sub-10% pieces. The sharp who carries a dart carries one of these two, because a hit at 5–8% ownership in a 784/1,189 field separates instantly (the article: Nicoll "in a win I would bet he's optimal").

## Key themes

- **This is a favorites-convert card with a ceiling problem at the top.** Five fighters are 76%+ to win (DailyFan), but the articles cap almost every one of them — Coria 100–110, Kline 80–90, Barbosa ~100 without ground-and-pound, DDP 90–100 — while the genuine 130+ ceilings live one tier down (Hooper, McMillen, Bashi, the HW fight). The winning score gets built from if-win points, not win probability.
- **The main disagreements are DailyFan-vs-itself, and they mark the pressure points**: the sheet's 24% on Franco vs the article's "might be low owned"; the article simultaneously calling Nicoll a small-portfolio fade and "optimal in a win"; Brett being "even higher on McMillen than Tim" while projecting him to chalk; and the article ranking Elliott's ceiling high while wondering if the field would over-own him (the sheet confirms: 29% vs Anderson's 25%).
- **Structurally this is the Bautista/Ofli setup again**: your last two slates were decided by a 5–16%-owned converter the strategy either skipped or faded, your leverage capture is 100% → 60% → 0%, and tonight's board offers exactly two sub-10% candidates (Nicoll, Melisano) plus a 13–15% live-dog shelf (Ramirez, Cannonier, Ricci). The screen above exists so none of them goes unconsidered.

## How to approach the slate

Two bullets, two distinct theses — per the SE framework, they must differ on at least one conviction anchor, not just the value slot. The 784-field Flying Knee is top-heavy ($2K to 1st): that's the bullet for the maximum-ceiling, leverage-carrying build — the recent-winner shape is ~35–40% average ownership with zero losing slots and ONE low-owned converter (your 7/12 winner: six fighters at 96–137 points, one 15%-owned definer, no darts wasted). The 1,189-field Clinch pays flat: a tight high-floor-of-ceiling chalk build competes fine there, so the differentiation budget belongs disproportionately to the top-heavy contest. Sharp-envelope target: all-unique, an elite if-win anchor (the Hooper/Barbosa tier rather than defaulting to the 45% main event), roughly one sub-15% piece per bullet — the sharks on your shared MMA slates carried a sub-5% dart in ~15% of lineups while you've carried one in 0%, and `own_per_slot` has been your biggest structural gap vs the pros in 2 of your last 4 slates; breaking the DDP+McMillen ~19% pairing is the single cheapest ownership discount on the board.
