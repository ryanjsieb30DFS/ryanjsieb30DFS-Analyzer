## The 4 decisions that define this slate

### 1. The captain slot IS the slate — PASS the expensive-favorite captains, PLAY Hokit + dog captains
**Mechanism:** in captain mode the 1.5× salary tax means an expensive favorite has to *out-score the whole board by 50%* to justify the slot — and the chalk already owns him in flex, so captaining him is double-paying. Both SIN authors independently land here: **Topuria optimal at CPT ~5%** (and chalky when he is), **Ruffy optimal at CPT <1% even when he scores 100+**, O'Malley capped (distance fight). Hokit is the one premium CPT worth paying — elite floor *and* ceiling in a win (9.25 SLpM, 3.89 TD), and most likely fighter to simply score the most points.
- **PLAY Hokit CPT (chalk-correct anchor).** If played → CPT $13,500, leaves **$36,500 for 5 flex ($7,300/slot)**. Comfortable: e.g. Topuria + Lopes + Garcia + a $5–6K dog. This is your "good chalk" bullet — most of the field's winning lineups run it, so 2–3 of your 10 should, differentiated downstream.
- **PLAY a dog captain for leverage (Garcia / Lewis / Daukaus, Gaethje/Chandler with eyes open).** The dogs are the *value* captains: they win more often than their CPT-ownership and the salary they save flows into a loaded flex. If played → CPT prices are cheap (Garcia $9.9K, Lewis $9K, Chandler $7.5K, Gaethje $8.1K), so you can run **Hokit + Topuria + Lopes** underneath. **Large field (5,945) makes the KO-or-bust dog captains correct** — the binary-leverage-is-weak lesson applies to *small* fields, not this one.
- **PASS Topuria / Ruffy / O'Malley AT CAPTAIN.** If you captain one anyway → only do it in 1 differentiation bullet (Ruffy as the pivot-off-Topuria), never as your core, and know you need an early KO + 100+ just to be optimal.
- **If you faded Hokit CPT entirely →** you'd be betting the slate's safest 90+ ceiling busts; price that — it's a ~24% fade (he loses) wearing the cost of also being right on a cheaper captain. Don't fade him in *all* 10; reserve him for 2–3.

### 2. Garcia / Lopes is the leverage fight — stack it (PLAY)
**Mechanism:** closest fight on the card (-155/+135), both elite finishers (Garcia 15/15 wins by KO; Lopes 23/27 finishes, 12 subs), line moved *toward* Garcia all week, and it stays lower-owned than the co-main. SIN: when **Garcia wins he's optimal more than anyone on the slate** and **~50% to be the optimal CAPTAIN** when those priced below him lose; **Lopes in a win can post 115+ and beat Hokit's ceiling.** This is exactly the cheap-low-own-finisher tier the 5/30 Macau lesson says to reserve a lineup for.
- **If played (Garcia) →** he's the best leverage captain on the board: CPT $9.9K, win equity *and* cheapest-winner upside, only 41% overall / much lower at CPT. Build Garcia-CPT + Hokit + Topuria + Bo/Lopes + value.
- **If played (Lopes) →** ceiling flex or CPT ($12.6K); pairs cleanly with a Hokit CPT.
- **Fight-stack expression:** with 6 picks from 7 fights, stacking the loser-still-scores fights is +EV. Garcia + Lopes both on one roster wins if it's a back-and-forth war (knockdowns/takedowns both ways) — SIN's single favorite stack "at ownership." Reserve **≥1 of the 10** for this stack (field-fade tier).
- **If faded →** you're betting the slate's most live cheap-finisher fight produces nobody optimal — historically the exact miss (Macau: Amorim/Vera). Don't.

### 3. Gane / Pereira co-main — over-owned pick'em → **Anchor-Equivalence** (MIX, underweight)
**Mechanism:** 5-round HW title, big names, mid-pricing → the field piles in (Gane 48% / Pereira 52%, the chalkiest pair on the board), but it's a literal coin flip (both 50%) and likely fought at distance (over 2.5 at -160). Captaining the winner is sneaky-good because the field over-owns the *fight* in flex but under-*captains* it; raw-stacking both sides at 40/40 burns lineups in a fight you think is over-owned. **This is the mandatory Anchor-Equivalence call:** Gane and Pereira are equivalent chalk at ~equal own.
- **How to satisfy it:** across the 10, rep **both** sides (you can't know the winner) **and do not captain the same side in every lineup** — e.g. 1–2 lineups Gane-CPT, 1–2 Pereira-CPT, several with neither (pure underweight to differentiate, per SIN's "none at CPT in 20-entry" stand).
- **If played →** treat as a cheap-ish title-fight flex/CPT; the *winner* CPT is the cheapest-winner edge. **If faded (in a given lineup) →** you need a non-co-main fighter to out-score whichever of Gane/Pereira hits — very live given both project ~62–69, so fading is a real differentiation lever, not a punt.

### 4. You need 2+ dogs — mine the right ones (MIX), and don't mistake salary-chalk for leverage
**Mechanism:** captain-mode salary math forces multiple cheap fighters into every roster, and you're guaranteed 2 winners below Hokit. The trap: **Gaethje (44%) and Chandler (44%) are NOT leverage** — they're salary-relief *chalk* the whole field uses to "make lineups work." The genuine low-owned dogs are **Garcia (win equity), Daukaus (SIN's pick-to-win, 31%), Lewis (25%, KO-or-bust CPT)**, plus **Zahabi (30%)** as the only dog with a *decision floor* (~33 in a loss could be optimal if the favorites all finish).
- **If played (Daukaus) →** he's the contrarian dog with the best actual win shot — SIN picks him over Bo. Live CPT ($9.3K) or flex; Bo's line is wide (-300 from -370).
- **If played (Zahabi) →** floor-flex only; the one bottom-tier piece that scores in a *loss*. Pairs with a finish-heavy core when you want a salary saver that won't zero.
- **If played (Gaethje/Chandler) →** fine as flex salary relief, but at 44% they don't differentiate — captain them only as a contrarian dog-CPT bullet, where their low *CPT* ownership (not flex own) is the edge.

## Where I disagree with the vendors
_Single projection vendor (DailyFan) + SIN qualitative; no MMA `vendor_calibration.jsonl` yet, so these are mechanism calls, not calibration-weighted (small-sample guard: 0 calibrated slates = note only)._

- **The bundle's leverage table crowns Ruffy (84.9 proj, lev-score 3.03) and O'Malley (lev-score 3.12) as the top leverage plays.** My call: they're **FLEX-only** leverage, and using them at **captain is a trap** the raw projection hides. Mechanism: the projection scores their *points*, not their captain *value* — at $15K/$13.8K CPT they have to lap the field, and SIN's sims have Ruffy <1% / O'Malley capped (distance) at CPT. Take the low own in flex; refuse the captain.
- **DailyFan win prob has Bo Nickal 74% to Daukaus 26%.** I shade toward **Daukaus being live** — SIN explicitly picks him to win (good everywhere, BJJ neutralizes Bo's wrestling, line bled from -370 to -300). Daukaus at 31% is the better contrarian dog than the price implies.
- **Projections + field treat Gane/Pereira as premium co-main plays (48/52% own).** I **fade the fight in flex** (coin flip fought at distance, over-owned because of names+5rds), while flagging the **winner-at-CPT as underowned** — the field's flex chalk doesn't carry to the captain slot. That's the edge inside the disagreement.
- **No disagreement on the top of the board:** Topuria (90) and Hokit (85) are correctly the two highest — I just split their *usage* (Topuria flex-only, Hokit the premium captain).
