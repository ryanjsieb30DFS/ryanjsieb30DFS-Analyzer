**1. Gable Steveson** ($9,900, 34% per DailyFan) — *Core* — The build-around anchor and best play on the slate (-3000, most likely to win, arguably the highest ceiling): a first-30-seconds takedown and pound-out puts 100+ on the board, and both writers call him the most-likely-optimal premium. Risk: 9.9k has been optimal just 1 of the last 6 times per MMA Labs, so he wins but may not be *optimal* — that price paradox is the leverage door.

**2. King Green** ($7,800, 28% per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — The slate's best leverage-plus-conviction blend: BOTH Technical Tim and Gordo pick Green outright in a -1200-to-finish fight, so he captures the same near-certain early finish as 46%-owned McKinney at ~100 pts in a win, 18 points of ownership cheaper. Risk: zero floor — the likeliest single outcome is he dies in two minutes, but that variance is the point at 28%.

**3. Alessandro Costa** ($9,100, 20% per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — The article-preferred low-owned finisher: -235 with wins that scored 107.8/110.3/102.5/107.5, and Brett explicitly leans him ahead of Riley and Yanez for a mid-round KO of the "essentially retired," recently-dropped Durden. Risk: KO-dependent — an extended fight where Durden grinds top control caps his ceiling.

**4. Terrance McKinney** ($8,400, 46% per DailyFan) — *Core* — Highest early-finish equity on the board: -1200 fight, 17 of 18 wins by RD1 finish, 115.7 avg in wins, "could be the most likely favorite to be optimal in a win." A finish-capable favorite worth owning despite the chalk. Risk: a coin flip dressed as chalk (fallen to -130) — his entire case is RD1, and if it extends "he very likely loses."

**5. Max Holloway** ($9,000, 45% per DailyFan) — *Core* — The safest premium floor with true slate-breaking upside (past wins of 209/164/153, ITD -140, mid-round TKO prediction). Highest-floor anchor. Risk: chalkiest play on the slate and the biggest duplication magnet — the articles flag that a ~105 score may not even be optimal on a slate this finish-heavy, so pivot to Steveson when you can.

**6. Lone'er Kavanagh** ($8,800, 17% per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — The article's named "8k contrarian target": a -220 favorite with real one-shot power who can reproduce the Manel Kape blueprint and KO Royval early for a low-owned ceiling. Risk: an extreme boom/bust — his ceiling is almost entirely an early KO, and "if the fight extends, I believe he is likely to bust."

**7. Paddy Pimblett** ($7,700, 29% per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — The leverage side of the main-card grappling fight: both writers lean Pimblett to WIN (better submission grappler, tougher, "weathers storms") while the projection favors BSD, and he's $800 cheaper. Risk: could be held down early, come back to win, and never reach a ceiling — a couple of ways this fight underperforms no matter who wins.

**8. Benoit Saint Denis** ($8,500, 36% per DailyFan) — *Core* — One of the best DK scorers in the sport (123 avg per win); a grappling-storm pace with backtakes and ground-and-pound gives a top-tier ceiling in a win. Risk: both matchup writers pick against him — his 42% striking defense and front-runner tendency mean Pimblett can break him if the early grappling doesn't land.

**9. Damian Pinas** ($8,900, 26% per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — A 100% finish rate (8 KO / 1 sub, 7 first-round) and a -400 ITD fight make him a pure RD1-KO threat; his last finish scored 119. Brett names him a preferred affordable path to an early KO. Risk: green and unproven — taken down by lesser regional wrestlers before, so if Almeida drags it long the power question opens up.

**10. Ryan Gandra** ($8,600, 29% per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — A strong tournament target in a -600-to-finish fight: back-to-back RD1 KOs, 13 strikes/min, a 130-point recent box score, and Reese has been KO'd early multiple times. Risk: reckless and hittable himself — "boom or bust," and Reese is the more well-rounded, technical fighter who can win minutes if it extends.

**11. Adrian Yanez** ($9,400, 17% per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — A low-owned favorite finisher (-450) who should out-throw the regressed, run-away Garbrandt and can score 110+ in a RD1 KO to leapfrog the premiums. Risk: expensive and boom/bust — poor defense means he can get caught early, and an extended slow fight may not produce enough offense to justify 9.4k.

**12. Cesar Almeida** ($7,300, 15% per DailyFan) — *Dart* — One of Brett's favorite sneaky dogs: a legendary GLORY kickboxer (+175, +225 ITD) with real counter power who has NEVER been KO'd, live to crack the slowing Pinas in a striking match. Risk: at 38 and taken down 20 times in 6 UFC fights on 52% TDD, he can get held on the mat and controlled if Pinas wrestles.

**13. Robert Whittaker** ($8,700, 16% per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — A low-owned (semi-contrarian) former champ the articles trust to WIN a technical striking affair and possibly hurt the slower, more predictable Krylov. Risk: boom/bust at 205 — he doesn't force exchanges, his previous two wins scored in the 60s, and the size/durability question is real, so he needs the KO to pay off the price.

**14. Zach Reese** ($7,600, 27% per DailyFan) — *Dart* — A cheap live finisher (80% finish rate, 7 first-round) whom Brett calls the higher-upside side over Krylov, with KO-and-sub paths in a -600 fight. Risk: durability — KO'd in RD1 twice and hurt early against McVey, so the more-powerful Gandra can "find the off switch" first.

**15. Farid Basharat** ($9,600, 15% per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — The highest-floor contrarian in the low-owned tier: -650 with a grappling/top-control path (3–5 takedowns projected) and shown ceilings of 110–120 twice, at a big ownership discount. Risk: expensive and largely decision-based — median wins are 80–90, and he's been out-struck late, so his ceiling isn't guaranteed to clear the mid-range at 9.6k.

**16. Nikita Krylov** ($7,500, 29% per DailyFan) — *Dart* — Cheap value (+105, "best value on the slate") with a 94% finish rate that makes him a live kill-or-be-killed piece in a -160 fight. Risk: likely chalkier value that could get counter-KO'd early — he's looked slow and been KO'd in RD1 in both 2025 fights, and Whittaker wants to slow the pace.

**17. Luke Riley** ($9,300, 11% per DailyFan) — *Dart* — The lowest-owned favorite on the board (-260), a fast, undefeated kickboxer who wins rounds by attrition — pure differentiation if he beats Kamaka. Risk: capped ceiling — +185 ITD points to a decision, his wins scored only 87 and 72, and even the writers "would rather play Yanez," so he's a leverage diff, not a smash.

**18. Tracy Cortez** ($8,000, 21% per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — A wrestling-base secondary who can grind top control for 10 minutes and push ~90 in a dominant decision to contend for the optimal. Risk: low finishing equity (+550 ITD) and only 86.9 avg per — if she can't stay committed to takedowns she loses to the better striker, and 80-point wins don't separate.

**19. Cory Sandhagen** ($8,300, 18% per DailyFan) — *Dart* — A skilled high-pace striker who "could sneak into the 90s and contend if others in this range fail," best when you need salary relief. Risk: a distance-leaning fight (-230) with no clear domination path — the most fair outcome is a semi-competitive decision that busts the price.

**20. Conor McGregor** ($7,200, 28% per DailyFan) — *Dart* — A low-end upside dart whose only real path is an early KO blitz in rounds 1–2 (+190, +215 ITD), with a name-value ceiling if he lands. Risk: overowned for his worth — five years off, a cardio question over five rounds, and the articles think Holloway wins at a very high rate.

**21. Mario Bautista** ($7,900, 17% per DailyFan) — *Dart* — A high-pace, well-rounded volume fighter (+116) who can land 80–100 strikes and mix in takedowns for ~80 in a win. Risk: a low ceiling — "no clear path to dominate," +500 ITD, and ~80 points at 7.9k rarely contends for the optimal.

**22. Cody Durden** ($7,100, 13% per DailyFan) — *Dart* — A wrestle-grind path (+200, 3.99 TD/15) that can produce a mid-80s decision if he lands early takedowns on Costa. Risk: gets finished a lot (submitted five times, recently KO'd twice), and recent wins capped at 89/84/86 — a low-end target, not a ceiling play.

**23. Wang Cong** ($8,200, 16% per DailyFan) — *Dart* — A technical kickboxer (6.22 strikes/min) with a low-owned contrarian pivot appeal if she keeps it standing and does damage. Risk: the article's lean is a BUST — needs knockdowns/a KO she rarely produces (+285 ITD), and Cortez taking her down early likely caps her even in a comeback win.

**24. Brandon Royval** ($7,400, 15% per DailyFan) — *Dart* — A volume-spam upset path: if he chases Kavanagh down and throws 100+ per round he can steal a pace decision or catch a tired Kavanagh. Risk: low-floor AND low-ceiling — +450/+500 ITD, eats strikes at 43% defense, and even 100 landed strikes is only ~70 DK points, a "mediocre play" the writers won't roster much.

**25. Kai Kamaka III** ($6,900, 14% per DailyFan) — *Dart* — A cheap, experienced round-winning kickboxer (+220) who "at least has a path to winning" and can score ~40 even in a loss if all the mid-range dogs miss. Risk: a low-upside sizable dog (+575 ITD) with no guaranteed floor or ceiling — a salary-relief punt only.

**26. John Garza** ($6,600, 7% per DailyFan) — *Dart* — A sub-10%-owned deep-field leverage punt: a well-trained boxer (+475) with a legit path on the feet if he can turn it into striking exchanges. Risk: on one week's notice vs a -650 grappler who wants 3–5 takedowns, his win equity is too thin for a 291-field SE — a large-portfolio dart, not a core play.

**27. Cody Garbrandt** ($6,800, 9% per DailyFan) — *Dart* — A sub-10%-owned punt whose only ceiling path is a fluke RD1 KO with his remaining fast-twitch power (+350). Risk: regressed and run-away style, lowest volume in the division, +550 ITD, and a "win" decision scores only ~42–50 — not enough to define the field. Punt-only.

**28. Elisha Ellison** ($6,300, 2% per DailyFan) — *Dart* — A sub-10%-owned massive-field dart: +1500 vs Steveson, and if he wins it's a quick KO worth a slate-breaking swing at 2% ownership. Risk: "among the worst fighters taped in the last decade," 8% win equity, zero ability to fight through adversity — a hard pass for a 291-field SE, only justifiable in a large-portfolio GPP.
