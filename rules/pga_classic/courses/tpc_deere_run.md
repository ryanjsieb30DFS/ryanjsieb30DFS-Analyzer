# TPC Deere Run — Silvis, IL (John Deere Classic)

**UNVERIFIED — built from this slate's articles only (2026-07-01, JDC 7/2/2026). Verify against results after the autopsy.**

- **Design/par:** D.A. Weibring, opened 2000, par 71, ~7,300 yards. Bentgrass throughout; greens among the purest/most receptive on Tour. Rolling Midwestern ground above the Rock River, tree-lined but generous.
- **Archetype: birdie-fest.** By ETR's numbers the **second-easiest course on the entire calendar**, plays well under par every year. Wide fairways (among the most generous on Tour), field hits a ton of greens, below-average rough/penalty profile, softest par-3/par-4 sets they have data on, all par-5s reachable. Winner is the aggressive scorer who goes on a heater, not the steady par machine. "Anyone can win" week.
- **Skill bias:** short irons over mid/long; light course-fit adjustments. Where present, ETR leans **driving accuracy over distance** + a slight **putting/around-the-green** emphasis (more tightly-mown areas than most venues). Ideal profile: hit fairways, go lights-out with the approach game, hot putter on pure greens.
- **Winning scores (recent):** 2025 Brian Campbell −18 (playoff over Grillo); 2024 Davis Thompson −28 (record); 2023 Sepp Straka −21; 2022 J.T. Poston −21; 2021 Lucas Glover −19. Expect low-20s under par; −28 not impossible with a stronger-than-usual field.
- **Weather (this slate):** hot, low 90s, high humidity, near-zero rain in practice days, small chance of a PM storm once play begins. Heat can firm the greens by the weekend if storms hold off. Wind impacts modeled as minimal this week (±0.4 pts by tee block; AM waves marginally favorable).
- **Field note (this slate):** strongest Deere in ages (8 top-50 players) but still much weaker than the surrounding elevated events — the Europe/Open swing pulls the game's best away. Ownership spreads out; little condensed chalk.
- **DFS read:** classic value/leverage week — the top of the field doesn't separate much from the $7K tier, so a low-priced, low-owned player breaking the slate is more likely here than almost anywhere. Course history is a real but light input; gate cheap CH plays on skill/form (per lessons.yaml).

_Sources: ETR Course Fits 7.2.26, ETR Large Field 7.2.26, ETR Value Report 7.2.26, ETR Models 7.2.26, The Stone 7/2/2026._
