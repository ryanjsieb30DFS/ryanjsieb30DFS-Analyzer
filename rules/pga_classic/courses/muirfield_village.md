# Muirfield Village Golf Club — Dublin, OH

*Created 2026-06-10 by the post-autopsy review for the Memorial Tournament slate (6/4/2026); physical profile sourced from that slate's articles via the archived slate analysis.*

## Physical profile

- Par 72, 7,569 yds. Hosts the Memorial Tournament (signature event **with a cut** — T-50 + ties / within 10).
- One of the hardest non-major tests on Tour: ~1.2 strokes over par adjusted since 2015.
- **Missed-fairway penalty top-5 on Tour**; GIR near the bottom 15%; firm/fast Augusta-like greens.
- Winner profile: elite tee-to-green ball-striker who avoids the big number — "limit the damage, let the field beat itself."
- Key skill tests: total driving (accuracy-weighted), approach, around-the-green.
- **Course-history stickiness: 0.42** — clearly above-average predictive (vs. Aronimink 0.11, Quail Hollow 0.48).
- Muted scoring → position points are a larger share of the win → winning the tournament matters more, strengthening elite anchors.

## Per-slate observations

- **2026-06-10 (Memorial 6/4/26, PGA Classic — SE 3,431 + 20-Max 23,781):** The elite-ball-striker / elite-anchor thesis **held at the top of the board** — Scheffler and Rory anchored nearly all SE top-10 and 20-Max top-10 winners (winning scores 534 SE / 559.5 20-Max). But the 0.42 stickiness did **not** rescue low-skill course-history darts: the CH-boost × sub-5% qualifiers (Vegas, Echavarria, Horschel — all bottom-10 skill baselines) all busted. The slate-defining cheap/mid plays came from form/value signals instead: J.T. Poston 115.5 @ 2.7%, Ryan Gerard 110 @ ~11%, Wyndham Clark 102 @ 7.5%, Kristoffer Reitan 96.5 @ ~11%, Eric Cole (3.5% own, in the 20-Max winner). Winning structure: SE top-34 averaged 12.25% own with 2.24 sub-10% plays per lineup; 20-Max top-100 averaged 12.59% own. Takeaway: at Muirfield, course history adds weight **on top of** a real skill baseline; it does not convert bottom-tier ball-strikers into viable darts at a course this penal.
