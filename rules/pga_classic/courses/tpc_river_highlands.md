# TPC River Highlands — Cromwell, CT (Travelers Championship)

**VERIFIED — 1 in-repo autopsy (2026-07-01 Travelers). Archetype confirmed by results; see per-slate observations below.**

Par 70, ~6,800 yds — **one of the shortest courses on Tour**, and that is the headline, not a footnote. Pete Dye design (early '80s) reworked by Bobby Weed; bunker overhaul 2016. Bentgrass tee-to-green, bluegrass/fescue rough. Signature closing stretch (15–18) wraps water in an amphitheater.

## Archetype: precision birdie-fest, NOT a bomber's track
- **Distance is a headwind, not a disqualifier.** Many players hit less than driver off several tees (position over power). Long hitters can still win (Bubba won 3×) but face a relative disadvantage vs a typical Tour week.
- **The separator is approach** — wedges/short irons into smaller-than-average greens. Winner archetype = sharp iron player who keeps a clean card and *capitalizes*, not the most conservative man. "You win by going low."
- **Missed-fairway penalty + penalty rate rank among the highest on Tour** — short yardage is NOT a license to spray. Must drive it in play (accuracy), just not far.
- Birdie barrage history: Furyk's 58 (2016, PGA Tour record) and Cameron Young's 59 (2024) — first Tour event with multiple sub-60 rounds.

## Course-fit signal (what to boost)
Accurate drivers + elite approach/birdie-makers. ETR course-fit boosts this slate: **Aaron Rai (#1), Lucas Glover, Ryan Gerard, Daniel Berger, Brian Campbell, Alex Fitzpatrick, Russell Henley, J.T. Poston, Corey Conners, Tom Hoge.** Bombers get a relative dock.

## 2026-06-24 (Travelers — Signature Event, no-cut, 72-man, 23 of top 25 OWGR)
- **Conditions tip to the birdie-fest end:** wet practice days (Mon/Tue rain) then dry/hot tournament days → soft, receptive greens. Low scoring expected; leaderboard bunches deep under par if wind stays down.
- **Minimal wave/wind edge** (ETR wind impacts all within ±0.3 DK pts; 72-man field, no real split per ETR panel).
- **Model-vs-narrative tension to watch:** ETR model *docks* the course history of Brian Harman and Keegan Bradley (2× winner, local "home" narrative) even as the ETG panel calls them northeast/course-history guys — narrative chalk the model distrusts.
- **Dose-darts read:** birdie-fest → ~2 sub-10% darts (per dose-darts lesson); no-cut removes the cut-governor so the dart count can run slightly higher without binary cut-out risk.

## Per-slate observations (post-autopsy, date-stamped)

### 2026-07-01 (Travelers — autopsy of the 2026-06-27 no-cut Signature Event)
- **Birdie-fest + archetype CONFIRMED.** Winning DK totals 681 (Albatross 3,431) / 690.5 (Dogleg 1,766) over the no-cut event — very high, deep-bunched leaderboard, exactly the soft/receptive/low-scoring read. The defining scorers were precision/approach ball-strikers, not bombers: **Viktor Hovland 144.0, Collin Morikawa 124.0, Matt Fitzpatrick 121.0, Akshay Bhatia 115.0, Corey Conners 114.5, Hideki Matsuyama 104.0.** Accuracy + elite approach won; the distance-headwind read held (Gotterup/Hojgaard appeared in a couple top-10 tails but were not definers).
- **Elite ball-striking talent > the vendor course-fit list.** Of the ETR course-fit boosts, only **Conners** (on the list) was an actual definer (114.5 @ 7.3%); **Poston and Rai (#1 fit) — both on the list — busted**. The two biggest scores (Hovland, Morikawa) were NOT on ETR's course-fit list at all — they won on raw skill. Read the fit list as a tiebreaker among comparable talent, not as a source of definers; do not let it crowd out top-of-board elites.
- **Dose-darts (no-cut birdie-fest):** winners ran ~2.5 sub-10% pieces (Albatross top-34 mean 2.56; Dogleg top-20 2.5) — a touch above the ~2 birdie-fest default, consistent with the no-cut format removing the binary cut-out governor. Target ~2-2.5 here, not a flat 2.
- **Chalk that HIT:** Morikawa was near-ubiquitous value chalk (~18% own, in ~60-65% of top lineups) — an all-fade-the-studs approach was a structural non-starter. **Narrative "trap" fades misfired:** Brian Harman and Keegan Bradley (both docked on CH by the model and faded pre-slate) each appeared in multiple winning lineups (Harman in the Dogleg overall winner) — they were fine complementary pieces, not the traps the strategy called; the model-dock did not warrant zeroing them.
- **Our result:** 98.3 / 99.7 pct (worst of the sample). Structure was on-envelope (13.9% avg own, 2 sub-10%); the failure was player IDENTIFICATION — zero of the six definers rostered, and Hovland + Morikawa never even appeared in the strategy doc (single-vendor coffin/panel blind spot).
