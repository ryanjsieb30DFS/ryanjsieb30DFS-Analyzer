## The 4 decisions that define this slate

### 1. Scottie Scheffler — $14,900 · 24.3% · the stars-and-scrubs gate
**MIX (≤1 of 3 lineups).** Mechanism: he's the only "Lock" cut tier on the board (90% MC, 70.3 proj, 101 ceiling) and the universal #1 baseline — but $14.9K is a tax that forces the rest of the roster into the $5–6K cut-risk swamp on a course where the cheap tier zeroes out. He's also a **course-fit dock (#4)** and **majors dock (#5)**: this setup neutralizes his edge more than any other.
- **If played →** $35,100 for 5 spots ($7,020/spot) — a 14.9 + five $6–7K plays. You're betting the cheap pieces survive the cut, so pair him with **proven-floor $6–7K names (Kitayama, English, McNealy), NOT $5K darts**.
- **If faded (≥2 of 3) →** you need a non-Scottie top anchor to clear ~70 pts AND your saved ~$3–5K to buy two extra mid-tier multipliers. At a 24% chalk anchor the fade is cheap leverage. This is the writer's stance ("Scottie is a how-much-do-you-trust-the-cheap-players play, not a skill play").

### 2. The condensed $7K chalk — Si Woo ($7.2K/29.6%) & Burns ($7.7K/19.2%)
**Si Woo: MIX-underweight. Burns: PASS.** The slate's defining ownership call. ETR's coffin flags BOTH as huge overweights (Si Woo +21.9, Burns +21.5) — but that is **one uncalibrated vendor disagreeing with itself**, in low-buy-in contests drawing ETR's own subscribers, where actual own drifts UP toward the optimal exactly when the overweight stops paying (single-vendor-self-erosion lesson). Si Woo is **already the highest-owned player on the slate** with a poor major track record and no course-fit flag; a coffin "overweight" on a 30%-owned name is a costume.
- **Si Woo if played →** genuinely ~$1,500 underpriced, so 1 lineup at modest weight is defensible value chalk — but NOT a hammer. His cheap price is the value engine that funds a top anchor; don't pair him with Burns or Cantlay (writer's flagged $7K chalk-on-chalk combo).
- **Burns if faded →** the fade needs the world where a 19% "safe" play posts a ceiling at ownership — the worst kind of bet on a course full of double/triple bogeys. Writer is FULL FADE; I land ~PASS. **Si Woo + Burns is NOT a leverage spine** (~49% combined own = two correlated chalk bets, full bust correlation, zero duplication discount). Treat each standalone.

### 3. Anchor-Equivalence — the 19.5–24.3% top cluster, and Bryson as the alternative
**The mandatory call.** Scheffler/Rory/Fleetwood/Aberg/Xander/Young are six substitutable anchors at ~20–24% own; the field treats them as interchangeable. **≥1 lineup MUST run the alternative top anchor.**
- **The alternative → Bryson DeChambeau ($11,000 · 6.1% · course-fit boost #2 · 81.8 ceiling).** Uncontested leverage: a top-5 ceiling at 6% own with a real course-fit edge (length + the widest USO fairways reward his profile). One Bryson-anchored lineup captures the cluster's leverage while sitting on the cheapest-owned of the equivalent set.
- **Within the cluster, prefer the coffin-value half:** **Fleetwood ($9.7K, +15.9 coffin, 22.9%)** and **Aberg ($9.2K, +7.8, 21.5%)** over the coffin-FADE half **Young (−7.3)** and **Rahm (−11.4 — course-fit boost but ETR's biggest top-end fade)**. These are leans, not laws (uncalibrated coffin). Never two of Fleetwood/Aberg/Fitzpatrick together (writer flag + correlation).

### 4. Where the slate is actually won — the 10–16% value multipliers, skill-gated darts
**PLAY the value band; gate the darts.** The last two autopsies say it plainly: these contests are won by **full exposure to every mid-priced 100+-ceiling play in the ~10–16% own band**, not by anchor picks or deep darts alone. On a 0.06-stickiness course the field will chase links-pedigree narratives (Cam Smith, Uihlein, Rodgers) — that signal is noise; **reject CH darts and gate cheap plays on skill/form + cut survival.**
- **If played →** build the value spine first: **Kitayama ($6.5K/12.4%/+16.2 coffin), Harris English ($6.7K/11.1%/+6.4), McNealy ($6.8K/11.2%/+6.9), Spaun ($7.5K/11.2%/+13.1, defending champ).** These are the points-per-dollar engines that let you afford Bryson/Fleetwood up top.
- **Darts (tough course → ~2–3, skill-gated) →** rotate **JT ($8.3K/7.5% — majors dock but elite ball-striker), Hatton ($8.7K/9.2%/course-fit #8), Hovland ($8.1K/9.4%), Koepka ($9.4K/7.2% — 2018 Shinnecock champ).** Avoid the <40%-make-cut amateurs (Preston Stout 0.1% own / 38% MC) — on a penal cut course they can't post the round and zero your lineup. Smalley ($6.8K/4.0%) is the writer's flag-plant and the one defensible sub-5% with a recent PGA ceiling — a single-lineup dart, not a core.
