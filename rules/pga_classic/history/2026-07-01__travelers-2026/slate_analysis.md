## How to approach the slate
Winning shape = an **elite anchor + a clean-card, birdie-making spine of accurate iron players**, with the separation found in the $6K–$8K tier — exactly where this course's value lives and exactly the band the last three slates' definers came from. Soft + short + no-cut means floors rise and *ceiling* is the whole game; the most conservative roster loses. Pay up once at the top, then load accuracy/approach value.

Lean **slightly contrarian, not reckless** (1.8K/3.4K SE fields): ~12–14% average own per slot, **2–3 sub-10% pieces**, **≥1 sub-5% leverage play** (Ben James / Snedeker / Cole / Straka), both lineups unique and a *distinct angle* — not two versions of one thesis. The deliberate exposure design: Poston and Si Woo on both; Scottie on one and Tommy on the other (the equivalence split); rotate the cheap differentiators. Don't chase distance — bombers (Cam Young, Gotterup, Knapp, Hojgaard) carry a course headwind here.
