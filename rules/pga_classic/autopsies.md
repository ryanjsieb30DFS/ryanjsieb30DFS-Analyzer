### Carry-Forward Notes

1. **Aberg blind spot needs correcting.** When the analyst consensus offers a multi-anchor double-tap option (Xander OR Aberg as projection chalk), the framework should not zero out one of them across the entire portfolio. At minimum, one lineup should carry the alternative chalk anchor as a hedge against the specific top-end outcome that beat us.
2. **The JT/Bradley leverage spine concept is validated.** Two coffin-list plays at sub-15% combined ownership produced 164 pts. Worth treating this as a near-permanent feature when the data supports.
3. **Contrarian builds without spine support are fragile.** L4 stacked four mid-range contrarian picks with no leverage floor. If 3 of 4 bust, the lineup has no path. Future contrarian builds should always include at least one coffin-list-grade leverage piece as anchor.
4. **The 2/4 portfolio cap is correct policy** even though it cost Reitan upside this week. Portfolio risk management protects against the reverse case where a chalk lock bombs in all lineups.
5. **Track the "winner appeared in N of M winning lineups" stat each week.** When one player is in 4 of 4, that's a structural slate truth, not noise. Future autopsies should flag those players retroactively to study whether they were identifiable pre-slate.

### Failure Mode Classification

Per Philosophy Section 4:
- **Variance loss (~70% of the loss):** The Keegan/Hatton/Hovland leverage spine collapsing in unison. Three high-conviction sub-10% own plays all busting on the same Sunday is statistically unlikely but mathematically expected over a sample. The plays were correct.
- **Blind spot (~25% of the loss):** Alex Smalley + Aaron Rai. Both were identifiable via ETR's course history boost list cross-referenced with low projected ownership. Neither made it into meaningful exposure.
- **Process error (~5% of the loss):** Stacking 3+ correlated leverage plays in a single SE lineup created concentrated bust risk. The plays were individually defensible; the concentration was not. Worth flagging as a Framework update candidate.

### Carry-Forward Notes

1. **Course history boost + sub-5% projected ownership is a new required pre-slate scan.** This pattern produced both Smalley (#7 on the boost list, 10.9% own, 96.5 FPTS) and Rai (#8 on the boost list, 2.27% own, 117 FPTS) — the two most-impactful plays of the slate. Adding this scan to Framework Section 2.
2. **Multi-leverage-spine SE lineups carry correlated bust risk.** The Truist autopsy validated 2-leverage-spine pairings (JT + Bradley). The PGA Championship demonstrated that 3+ leverage plays in a single SE lineup is structurally over-concentrated. Pattern needs 2-3 more weeks of observation before becoming a hard Framework rule, but the cautionary note is being added to Section 5 immediately.
3. **The coffin chalk thesis at the top of the board validated cleanly.** Rahm, Aberg, Rory, Schauffele — all four top-tier coffin overweights hit. The value chalk vs trap chalk distinction worked exactly as designed. Hojgaard was median (correctly chalky), Kitayama was a strong hit (correctly chalky), Schauffele was a hit (overowned but performed).
4. **The Aberg blind spot from Truist is fully corrected.** Aberg at 30.7% MME exposure delivered 84 FPTS as the model #6 baseline and coffin +11.3%. The carry-forward note from Truist Week 1 worked.
5. **15-build optimizer iteration cycle was a process inefficiency.** Across 15 build iterations, the structural reads stabilized by Build 6. Builds 7-15 produced marginal noise rather than improvements. Going forward: two-build maximum per slate (one conviction baseline, one refined version). This is being formalized as Framework Section 11.
6. **Contest selection shift effective May 19, 2026.** Discontinuing 150-max MME as default workflow. Primary vehicle: Single Entry. Secondary: 3-max, 5-max. Occasional: 20-max and small-portfolio large-field MME. Framework Section 1 updated accordingly.
