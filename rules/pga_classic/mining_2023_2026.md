## Stability (the pattern is structural, not noise)
Winners' avg ownership barely moves year to year: **2023 12.9% · 2024 13.2% · 2025
12.8% · 2026 12.8%**; rank-1 sub-10% median 2–3 every year. Four seasons, same shape.

## Course archetype (winning-score tertiles: tough ≤591 · mid · birdie-fest ≥659)
| Archetype | n | Winners avg own | Winners <10% mean | Rank-1 sub-10% median |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tough | 57 | 12.8% | 2.59 | **3** |
| Mid | 55 | 13.1% | 2.49 | 3 |
| Birdie-fest | 56 | 13.1% | 2.16 | **2** |
Avg ownership is flat across archetypes, but **birdie-fests reward fewer darts** (median
2 sub-10% vs 3 on tough courses): when the field's chalk also scores, over-leveraging loses.

## The slate-defining low-owned play is near-universal
**96% of slates (161/168)** had ≥1 player who was rostered in ≥30% of winning lineups
while drawing <20% field ownership — **median 4 such plays per slate**, median ownership
**~12%**, median actual **108 FPTS**. There is essentially always a sub-radar golfer who
smashes and shows up across the winners; finding the leverage tier *is* the build.

## Candidate lessons (seeded into `lessons.yaml`)
1. **Winning structure ≈ 13% avg own + 2–3 sub-10% golfers, 91% unique** — 168-slate
   confirmation of the framework's leverage-spine / ownership-tier targets (the existing
   "12.25 / 2.24" SE reference holds at scale). *validated.*
2. **The leverage play is mandatory, not optional** — 96% of slates have a <20%-own
   player across the winners; an all-chalk lineup structurally cannot separate. *validated.*
3. **Dose darts to course variance** — birdie-fests: ~2 sub-10%; tough courses: ~3.
   *hypothesis* (proposed framework calibration).
