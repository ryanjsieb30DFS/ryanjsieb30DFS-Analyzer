# DFS Philosophy — Ryan's GPP Approach

## Core Identity

I am an experienced GPP-focused DFS golf player. I play to **win contests**, not to cash. Every decision is filtered through "does this increase the probability of a top-1% finish" — not "does this increase expected value" or "does this reduce variance."

Cashing is a byproduct of correct GPP construction, never the goal.

## The Six Foundational Rules

### 1. Leverage > Floor
The whole point of GPP is to be different from the field in ways that pay off. Safe plays at high ownership are anti-leverage. I'd rather be wrong on a contrarian thesis than right on consensus — being right on consensus pays the same as everyone else and doesn't win. I don't want to be different just to be different. If a chalk piece is a good play, we want to play said chalk piece, just how we build around chalk piece needs to be sharp. 

### 2. Every Lineup Needs a Thesis
If I can't articulate a one-sentence reason a lineup wins ("this is the no-top-3 build that wins if CY slips and the LB block gains 4-5 spots each"), the lineup is a portfolio filler and shouldn't be entered. Each lineup attacks the field from a distinct angle.

### 3. Every Lineup Needs a 60+ Ceiling Player
**Hard rule from Cadillac autopsy.** No lineup without at least one player with a realistic 60+ point ceiling can win a 5,000+ entrant Showdown contest. The math doesn't work. Anchor-of-the-week, cheap-spike-of-the-week, or low-owned mid-tier with breakout potential — every lineup needs one.

### 4. Coffin Avoidance Is Real
High-ownership plays at marginal price/profile are field traps. Maximum one coffin-list player per lineup. Stacking two coffin plays is a leverage death sentence — if they hit, the field hits with you; if they don't, you're buried.

### 5. Don't Punch Out of the Contest
With limited bullets (4-20), zero exposure to the modal anchor is too aggressive. Going under-weight is leverage. Going to zero is a voluntary lottery exit. Specifically: **never take any of the top-3 anchors to 0% exposure with 4 bullets**, especially when one is leading by 4+ shots.

### 6. The Slate Read Is Upstream of Everything
Before any lineup is built, the slate read must be locked: rule-set commitment, modal anchor decision, ceiling slot identification, leverage angle. When conviction is high, that read drives every lineup on the slate — one canonical build deployed across contests (Mode A). When conviction is low, the portfolio can carry 2-3 distinct reads as a hedge (Mode B), but each lineup must independently hit the structural floor (60+ ceiling player, sub-5% spike on birdie-fest slates, no mid-tier trap, no coffin stacking). What's never acceptable: lineups built without a deliberate read, where contest entry triggered the build rather than the build expressing a read.

## What I Push Back On

- **Tier organization by salary instead of conviction.** Tiers should reflect strategic role, not price band.
- **Over-broad "play the slate" recommendations** without specific exposure targets per lineup count.
- **Generic ownership reads** that ignore that ownership shifts as ETR subscribers consume the content. By Sunday morning, ETR-flagged value plays often have ownership that's already corrected.
- **Anchor bias from prior week — applies to both me and my analyst (Claude).** Players I or Claude discussed favorably last week are not default leans this week. Each slate gets re-read from scratch. A player's role this week is determined by this week's leaderboard, ownership, and rule-set — not by what was said about him seven days ago. Min Woo Lee in PGA Championship 2026 (featured 2 of 3 lineups, scored 30.55) was the exact pattern — carried over from Truist autopsy framing without re-validating against the new slate. **Autopsy language going forward references patterns that worked (cheap-spike-of-the-week profile, sub-25 climber on birdie-fest), not specific players who worked.** Specific player names in autopsies become default leans the following week; that's a leak.

## Format-Specific Mental Model

R4 Showdown is fundamentally a **probability distribution over leaderboard movement**, not a stat-projection exercise. The question isn't "who scores the most points" — it's "given starting position X, what's the realistic ceiling for movement up the board today, and how does that player's price/ownership compare to alternatives in the same tier?"

This is why ceiling > floor at every roster slot, and why "structural value" plays (sub-25 starter at $8K-$9K with single-digit ownership) are the most important slots in the lineup — they're the leverage spots where ceiling differential pays off most.

## On Structural Leaks (Golf)

**The Anchor-Equivalence Rule is a 3-slate confirmed leak (Cadillac → Truist → CJ Cup 5.24.26).** RD4 SD is a flat 6-golfer format (no captain slot); the anchor is the top-salary / top-projection golfer. When two chalk-tier alternatives exist at similar ownership (within ~15%), concentrating exposure on one is a structural error. **At least one lineup MUST run the alternative.** CJ Cup 5.24.26 specifically: 100% Scheffler at anchor / 0% Clark across 4 lineups despite both at chalk-tier own — Clark scored 97.8 (slate-defining), cost us the slate. This is a pre-lock enforced check, not a post-hoc lesson.

**Sim ROI as a selection ranker is suspect.** Sim ROI rank measures expected value across the full field — that's the *cashing* metric, not the winning metric. On certain slate types it can be anti-informative (top-by-sim picks scoring below pool mean). Treat Sim ROI as one input, never as the primary lineup-selection filter on RD4 SD. Confirm with post-sim data when available.

**Use `post_roi_pct` as gold-standard retroactive equity metric when available.** One-shot actuals can be variance; post_roi averages over many alternate fields with deterministic actuals to isolate selection skill from field luck. When reviewing any past slate, "did this lineup have real equity?" is answered by post_roi, not by the one real field outcome. (Note: post-sim wasn't run on CJ Cup 5.24.26 before pools cleared — this rule is provisional for golf-SD pending the next slate's confirmation.)
