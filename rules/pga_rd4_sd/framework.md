## The Build Workflow (Four Stages)

Every slate runs through this sequence. No lineup gets built before Stage 1 is complete; no lineup gets submitted before Stage 3 is verified.

### Stage 1 — Slate Read

Answer six questions before any lineup work:

1. **Rule-set call:** Birdie-fest, tough-course, or genuinely uncertain → split.
2. **Conviction level:** High (→ Mode A single canonical read) or Low (→ Mode B diversified multi-read).
3. **Modal anchor:** Identity and field ownership. Set exposure target.
4. **Sub-5% cheap-spike-of-the-week candidate:** Mandatory ID on any potential birdie-fest slate. Identify the way-back ceiling lottery play(s).
5. **Coffin block:** Players to avoid (rule-set conditional — strict avoid on tough-course, loosened on birdie-fest).
6. **Anchor-equivalent pairs:** For every analyst-named structural value pick at sub-25% own, identify the lower-owned same-profile alternative. Plan rotation.

### Stage 2 — Build

- **Mode A (high conviction):** One canonical lineup that expresses the read. Note any contest-specific variants needed for deployment.
- **Mode B (low conviction):** 2-3 lineups, each calibrated to a different scenario. Each independently complete.

### Stage 3 — Pressure Test (per lineup, before lock)

Every lineup individually passes this checklist:
- All structure targets hit (T-10 count, sub-25, sub-5%, sub-10%, cum own in range for rule-set)
- 60+ ceiling player present
- Sub-5% spike included if slate could play birdie-fest
- Mid-Tier Ownership Trap satisfied (≤2 plays at 10-20% own)
- No coffin stacking (rule-set conditional)
- Anchor-equivalence check completed
- Full salary used ($50,000 exactly or within $200)
- Articulable one-sentence thesis

**Portfolio-level check (across all lineups, not per-lineup):** Correct-Chalk Concentration — if a justified moderate-own core exists, ≥1 lineup rosters the full core (see section below).

### Stage 4 — Deploy

Match contest priority to lineup conviction. SE contests get strongest reads; MME fallback gets variants. Lock and submit.


## Rule-Set Decision Framework

The rule-set choice (birdie-fest vs tough-course) is the **highest-leverage decision of the build**. Get this wrong and the rest of the process can't save you (Cadillac 2026 and Truist 2026 both confirmed).

### Birdie-Fest Rules
- Min 3, max 4 of the T-10 leaderboard
- Higher cumulative ownership target (113%)
- Fewer sub-5% plays (0.79 avg)
- Lock in more chalk; sub-25 anchors matter less

### Tough-Course Rules
- Min 2, max 3 of the T-10 leaderboard
- Lower cumulative ownership target (100%)
- More sub-5% / sub-10% plays (1.06 / 2.17 avg)
- Sub-25 anchors are the structural value slots

### Decision Inputs (in priority order)
1. **Number of T-10 starters.** ~10 exact = tough-course lean. ~15+ separated by 1-2 strokes = birdie-fest lean.
2. **Day 3 scoring distribution.** If only 1-2 players in double digits to par on Saturday → tough-course.
3. **Course weather forecast for Sunday.** Wind/rain → tough-course; calm + soft → birdie-fest.
4. **Pricing dynamics.** If DK kept classic-style pricing into R4 (no major adjustments), there's more value at every tier — leans birdie-fest. If aggressive R4 pricing inflation on T-10 → tough-course.

### Critical Lesson From Cadillac → Truist 2026
**Rule-set splitting is the default for 4-bullet portfolios, not the exception.** Two consecutive R4 SDs lost at the rule-set commitment level — Cadillac with an uncertain analyst, Truist with a certain one. Analyst confidence is not sufficient evidence to commit; the analyst is reading the same noisy signals.

**New default: 2-2 split on rule-set for any 4-bullet portfolio.** Override to one-sided commitment only when there's overwhelming forcing evidence (severe weather, course setup change, scoring distribution physically constrained to one direction). The cost of being wrong with full commitment is too high to bear, and we've now demonstrated this twice in succession.


## Roster Construction Targets by Format

The rules below are calibrated to **handbuilt** play — every lineup is individually constructed and must earn its slot. This is not optimizer-driven portfolio construction. Each lineup needs an articulable thesis regardless of format.

### Single-Entry (1 Lineup)

**Foundational principle:** Your one lineup IS your strategy. There is no portfolio safety net. Every decision must survive on its own.

**Construction rules:**
- **Field-weight the modal anchor** — do not fade. If CY is at 45%+ in the field, you have CY. The exception is when there are two co-leaders both above 35% (coin-flip case) — pick one.
- **Cum own target: 105-115%.** Slightly above field average is correct in single-entry.
- **Hit every historical winning structure target** — non-negotiable: 2-3 T-10 starters (or 3-4 birdie-fest), 1+ sub-25 starter, 1 sub-10% play, 1 ceiling player (60+ realistic upside).
- **Exactly one calculated leverage bet** — placed in the cheapest 1-2 salary slots. Six leverage bets in one lineup is six ways to lose.
- **No coffin-block players.** Zero. Coffin players are field-weight without leverage benefit — wrong on both axes for single-entry.
- **Rule-set commitment required.** Cannot split with one lineup. If genuinely 50/50, lean tough-course.
- **Top 3 salary slots are not leverage slots.** $9,500+ slots get the modal anchor, a second T-10 starter, and a structural value sub-25 anchor.
- **Anchor-equivalence rule resolves to one pick.** Default to analyst-named (Sky's call). Override only on materially better factors. If genuinely indifferent, pick the lower-owned profile.
- **The Mid-Tier Ownership Trap rule applies: max 2 plays at 10-20% ownership per lineup.**

**Construction template:**

| Slot | Profile | Salary Range |
|------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | Modal anchor | $10,500+ |
| 2 | Second T-10 starter | $9,000+ |
| 3 | Structural value sub-25 anchor | $8,500-$9,500 |
| 4 | Mid-tier T-10 or sub-15% conviction | $7,000-$8,500 |
| 5 | Sub-10% leverage shot | $6,500-$7,500 |
| 6 | Min-priced punt with single-round upside | $6,000-$6,500 |

### Handbuilt MME (4-30 Lineups in 150-Max Contests)

**Foundational principle:** Handbuilt curated portfolio — not optimizer-driven volume. Each lineup must still earn its slot. Filler lineups are a leak, not a hedge. MME is the fallback when SE contests aren't offered or aren't adequate for the slate (e.g., contests under ~2,500 entries with multi-entry caps are functionally treated as SE per contest size — see Multi-Contest Slate Read Discipline below).

**Construction rules:**

**Universal (applies to any handbuilt MME count):**
- **Each lineup has its own one-sentence thesis.** Same standard as single-entry.
- **Top-3 anchor exposure: 25-60% range across portfolio.** Never 0%, never 100%.
- **Modal anchor exposure: under-weight by 10-20 points vs field.** If CY is 50% in the field, target 30-40% in your portfolio.
- **No two lineups share more than 4 of 6 players.**
- **Build first lineup as if it's your single-entry build** — set the structural floor, then differentiate from there.
- **Each subsequent lineup must materially differ** from prior lineups (different anchor, different rule-set, different leverage angle, or different sub-25 profile).
- **Rule-set splitting is the default on 4-bullet portfolios — 2-2 minimum unless overwhelming forcing evidence justifies one-sided commitment.**
- **The Mid-Tier Ownership Trap rule applies: max 2 plays at 10-20% ownership per lineup.**

**Cum own distribution across portfolio:**
- 4-6 lineups: range 90-125%
- 7-15 lineups: range 80-130%
- 16-30 lineups: range 75-135%

**Sub-tier specifics:**

**4-6 Lineups (small handbuilt MME — Cadillac / Truist case):**
- 1 flag-plant maximum (cum own 75-90%)
- All other lineups must hit historical winning structure targets individually
- Sub-5% / sub-10% targets met in aggregate, not every lineup needs them
- **Rule-set splitting required: 2-2 minimum on 4 bullets, regardless of analyst confidence.** Override only with overwhelming forcing evidence.
- If one lineup has 0% exposure to top-3 anchors, the rest of the portfolio must have 50%+ collective exposure

**7-15 Lineups (mid handbuilt):**
- 2-3 flag-plants allowed
- Rotate sub-5% punts across lineups — each unique leverage shot deserves only 1-2 lineups
- Anchor exposure can spread wider (e.g., 4 with CY, 3 with Scheffler, 4 with SWK, 4 with no top-3)
- Track running aggregate exposure: no non-anchor player should appear in >50% of lineups

**16-30 Lineups (high handbuilt — approaching optimizer territory):**
- Start managing aggregate exposure caps as your primary discipline
- Top-3 anchor exposure caps: 60% maximum on any single anchor
- Mid-tier exposure caps: 40% maximum on any single mid-tier player
- Sub-10% exposure caps: 25% maximum on any single sub-10% play (forces leverage rotation)
- 3-5 flag-plant lineups acceptable
- Discipline check: if you can't articulate why lineup #N is different from lineups #1-(N-1), do not enter it. Submit fewer lineups instead.

### The "Earn Its Slot" Discipline

Across all handbuilt formats, the core test for any lineup is: **"If this were the only lineup I entered, would I be comfortable with it?"**

If the answer is no, the lineup is filler. Filler lineups don't hedge — they dilute. Three high-conviction lineups beat ten low-conviction lineups in the same contest. Better to enter fewer lineups well than more lineups poorly.

## Multi-Contest Slate Read Discipline (From PGA Championship 2026)

When playing multiple contests on the same slate (e.g., two SE contests plus one small-field multi-entry treated as SE), the read mode determines workflow. Two valid approaches:

### Mode A: High-Conviction Single Read

Used when the slate has a clear read — obvious rule-set, single modal anchor, identifiable ceiling slot.

Workflow:
1. Establish the slate read first — rule-set, modal anchor, ceiling slot, leverage angle (Stage 1 of the build workflow)
2. Build the canonical lineup that expresses the read
3. Deploy that build (or close variants) across each contest
4. Strongest contest priority gets the canonical build; secondary contests get close variants

### Mode B: Diversified Multi-Read

Used when the slate is uncertain — rule-set ambiguous, anchor coin-flip case, multiple viable ceiling paths.

Workflow:
1. Identify the 2-3 scenarios the slate could unfold as (e.g., birdie-fest vs tough-course; modal anchor holds vs falls back)
2. Build one lineup per scenario, each calibrated to a different read
3. Each lineup must independently hit structural floor (see rule below)

### The Structural Floor (Both Modes — Non-Negotiable)

Regardless of Mode A or B, EVERY lineup entered must independently hit:
- 1+ realistic 60+ ceiling player
- 1+ sub-5% spike play if the slate could play birdie-fest (cheap-spike-of-the-week profile)
- Max 2 plays at 10-20% ownership (Mid-Tier Ownership Trap)
- Rule-set discipline (T-10 starter count, cum own target for the rule-set the lineup is built for)
- No coffin block stacking (rule-set conditional)
- Articulable thesis

**Failure mode (PGA Championship 2026):** Three diversified lineups (Mode B), zero structural floor hits on the sub-5% spike slot. Kitayama at 3.40% own / 72.25 pts was the birdie-fest cheap-spike-of-the-week and zero lineups had him. The diversification was valid; the structural rule miss was not. Mode B is a hedge against uncertain reads, not a license to skip rules on individual lineups.

### Contest Size Calibration

Small-field multi-entry contests (≤2,500 entries) are functionally SE for our purposes — the conviction level required and structural rule discipline apply the same as a true SE contest. The label on DraftKings doesn't change the build approach; field size and contest dynamics do.


## Coffin Block Identification (Per Tournament)

Coffin block = high-ownership plays at marginal price/profile. Identifiers:
- Ownership 20%+ AND
- One of: middle-LB position with shaky form, high salary at modest LB position, or analyst-flagged "field favorite" with thin underlying case

**Maximum one coffin-block player per lineup.** Stacking two is a leverage death sentence.

**Rule-set dependence (from Truist 2026):** Coffin avoidance is primarily a tough-course strategy. On birdie-fest slates, the "coffin block" often hits because cum own targets are naturally higher (113%) and the chalk represents accurate ownership concentration on real value. When playing birdie-fest rule-set lineups, the max-1 coffin rule loosens to max-2, and zero-coffin exposure is no longer required across the portfolio. The "coffin" label itself is rule-set-conditional — verify the play actually fits the coffin profile under the rule-set the lineup is built for. On birdie-fest the same high-owned mid-tier may simply be "the field correctly identifying real value."

## The Mid-Tier Ownership Trap (From PGA Championship 2026)

The 10-20% ownership tier is the most dangerous slot in a lineup. Plays in this range are field-weight without the structural leverage benefit of either:
- True chalk (20%+ on real ceiling shots that justify ownership)
- True leverage (sub-5% on cheap-spike-of-the-week profiles)

Stacking 3-4 mid-tier plays at 10-20% ownership in a single lineup means filling slots that don't differentiate from the field while also lacking ceiling. The lineup floor is okay; the lineup ceiling is capped because no slot is doing the leverage work.

**Rule: Maximum 2 plays at 10-20% ownership per lineup.** The remaining 4 slots should be a mix of:
- Top-3 anchors (20%+ own with conviction)
- Structural value mid-tier (analyst-named with 8-15% own and clear thesis)
- Sub-10% leverage shots (5-10% own with low-owned ceiling profile)
- Sub-5% spike plays (cheap-spike-of-the-week)

**Failure pattern (PGA Championship 2026):** Lineups filled with 4-5 plays in the 10-20% own range — all middling 17-33 pt outcomes, no slot doing leverage work, ceiling capped at ~210 total. Either commit higher (20%+ ceiling shots) or commit lower (sub-5% spikes). The middle is the trap.

## The Anchor-Equivalence Rule (3-slate golf confirmation)

**Confirmed across Cadillac → Truist → CJ Cup 5.24.26.** No longer suggestive — load-bearing pre-lock check.

For every analyst-named "structural value" anchor, identify the 1-2 equivalent-profile alternatives at the same ownership/salary tier. Rotate exposure across them rather than concentrating on the named pick.

**Equivalent profile = same salary tier (±$500), same ownership tier (±15%), similar starting LB position, similar realistic ceiling.**

- **Cadillac 2026:** analyst-named anchor and uncommented same-profile alternative had same salary tier, same ownership, same starting position. Field rotated; we concentrated on the named pick.
- **Truist 2026:** repeat — another analyst-named / equivalent-uncommented pair. Same miss.
- **CJ Cup 5.24.26:** RD4 SD is flat 6-golfer (no CPT slot); the anchor is the top-salary / top-projection golfer. Scheffler vs Wyndham Clark at similar chalk-tier own. Went 100% Scheffler / 0% Clark across 4 lineups. Clark scored 97.8 (slate-defining), Scheffler scored 50s. Best finish 4,560 of 8,323.

**Pre-lock check:** for every chalk-tier (~20%+ own) anchor candidate, run the explicit anchor-equivalence scan — identify the equivalent-profile alternative and verify ≥1 lineup runs it before locking. This is a hard checklist item, not a vibe check.

## The Correct-Chalk Concentration Rule (PGA RD4 SD 5.31.26)

**The near-inverse of Anchor-Equivalence, for a different slate shape.** Anchor-Equivalence governs the *coin-flip* case — two equivalent-profile chalk anchors, split exposure across them. Correct-Chalk Concentration governs the *shared-core* case — when 3-4 *justified* moderate-own plays (≈10-25% own, real ceiling, the field will largely share them) form the slate's correct core, **at least one lineup must roster the full core together.** Single legs of the core scattered across many lineups do not capture the win.

**Why it's a rule:** On RD4 SD 5.31.26 the winning lineup and the field's entire top-4 shared a four-play moderate-own core (each member overperformed projection by ~20 pts). Our 10-lineup portfolio held single legs scattered — **no lineup carried more than 2 of the winning 6** — and clustered just under the cash line. The slate's actual leverage was *connected correct chalk*, not contrarian fades.

**Pre-lock check (portfolio-level):**
1. Identify the justified moderate-own core — moderate ownership (≈10-25%), real ceiling, course/form-supported. Distinguish from *popular ceiling-light chalk* (the highest projected-own plays that are popular without a ceiling case — these are the coffin tier, not the core).
2. Verify ≥1 lineup rosters 3-4 members of that core *together*, not as scattered single legs.
3. Do not over-diversify: with a clear birdie-fest read, fewer distinct cores rostered deeply beats max breadth. A portfolio clustered just under the cash line is the symptom of dilution.

**Interaction with other rules:** This does not override the Mid-Tier Ownership Trap (≤2 plays at 10-20% own per lineup) — the trap targets *unconnected* mid-tier filler with no ceiling. A *justified, course-supported* moderate-own core that all profiles as ceiling plays is the opposite of trap filler. The distinction is ceiling and correlation, not ownership band. Still pair the core with the mandatory sub-5%/sub-10% spike for differentiation.

## Post-Slate Equity Metric

**Use `post_roi_pct` as the gold-standard retroactive lineup-quality metric when available**, not one-shot actual rank. One-shot actual rank is a single sample from the field-construction distribution and can be variance-distorted. The post-contest sim runs many alternate fields with deterministic actuals, isolating selection skill from field variance.

When reviewing any past golf slate, the question "did this lineup have real equity?" is answered by post_roi, not by the one real field outcome. Top-N by post_roi has been shown (on at least one slate elsewhere in the tool) to closely match top-N by actual when the slate plays to its equity-correct expectations — making post_roi the more stable signal.

Note: post-sim wasn't run on CJ Cup 5.24.26 before pools cleared, so direct golf-SD evidence is pending. Run post-sim on the next golf slate (in the Autopsy tab, with the inline checkbox checked) to start building the calibration record.

## The Ceiling-Player Requirement

**Every lineup needs at least one player with a realistic 60+ point ceiling.** This is a hard rule, not a guideline.

Ceiling-player identification:
- **Anchor-of-the-week** — the leader/co-leader in a position to post a clean 65-70+ round
- **Cheap-spike-of-the-week** — sub-$7K player at low ownership with course history of low rounds
- **Low-owned mid-tier breakout** — 7K-8K player at sub-15% ownership with single-round upside profile

If a lineup has zero realistic 60+ ceiling players, it is structurally incapable of winning a 5K+ entrant contest. Rebuild it before locking.

**Ceiling math is rule-set-dependent (from Truist 2026).** A T32 starter "can't realistically score 60+" only on tough course (winning at -15 to -17). On birdie-fest (winning at -19 to -22), -7 to -10 rounds are routine and way-back ceiling lottery plays (cheap-spike-of-the-week profile) have substantially better equity than position-based math suggests.

**When evaluating any ceiling player's realism, explicitly name the rule-set the math is assuming.** If the rule-set call is uncertain (which is the default per the splitting rule above), keep the ceiling exposure rather than rationalizing it out. Removing a sub-5% ceiling shot based on tough-course math when the slate plays birdie-fest is the exact pattern that cost us Truist.

**Sub-5% spike sub-clause (from PGA Championship 2026):** On major championship / birdie-fest slates, the portfolio must contain at least one sub-5% cheap-spike-of-the-week play across the build. The slate's three contest winners at PGA Championship 2026 all included the sub-5% spike (Kitayama at 3.40%). Each individual lineup needs a 60+ ceiling player; the portfolio needs at least one sub-5% spike across all lineups when birdie-fest is in play.
