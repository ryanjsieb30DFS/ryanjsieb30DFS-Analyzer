## Strategic notes (from 6.14.26 articles)

- **Two main components decide finishing order: (1) track position is king, (2) horsepower.** Drivers cannot win without track position; teams reach the front via qualifying OR unconventional pit strategy, so finishes can deviate sharply from running position (Larson led with 44 to go in 2024, sped on pit road, finished 13th; Bubba ran 18th in driver rating but top-10'd on fuel strategy).
- **Two-dominator builds are the most likely optimal construction.** 9 of last 11 (ex-doubleheaders) perfect lineups featured ≥2 drivers starting top-10; 26 drivers led ≥20 laps across those races, 73.1% of them starting 6th or better. Top-5 starters made up 31.5% of top-10 DK scores. A single-dominator build is only justified if an elite PD play competes with dominator points (2019-2: Larson/Byron PD plays beat fitting an $11k+ dominator).
- **Optimal starting-spot distribution (DailyFan):** Top-5 start 14.3% of optimal spots / Top-10 33.3% / 11th-20th 28.6% / 21st-30th 28.6% / worse-than-30th **only 9.5%**. The meat of optimal lineups is the **11th-30th start band (57%)**. Worse-than-30th is the *rarest* optimal bucket — deep-back PD is a real but thin path here, unlike a high-carnage intermediate.
- **Caution/DNF rate:** NextGen Pocono has run higher caution laps than pre-2016, but DNFs are low in normal years (2022/2023/2025 = 5/4/4 DNFs); 2024's 13-DNF race is the outlier. Best-ever finish from a worse-than-30th starter in NextGen = Justin Haley 22nd / 32 DK pts. Don't bank deep-back raw-PD ceilings the way you would at a chaotic 1.5-miler.
- **Practice speed correlates well for the TOP tier** (11 of top-15 2024 fantasy scorers ranked top-15 in 5-lap practice; Byron 1st in 5-lap in 2023 led 60 laps). For the **value/lower tier, practice speed is treacherous** — the field over-owns value plays on practice speed and the actual value scorers come from lower ownership (2024: highest-owned value plays Gilliland 27%/6pts, Stenhouse 21%/6pts, McDowell 20%/15pts all busted; highest-scoring value were Erik Jones 8%/38, Lajoie 11%/35, Haley 17%/32). **Use the field's practice-speed reliance as the value-tier leverage point.**
- **Historical optimal PD hits** came from elites starting deep, not value-tier deep starters: Byron P31→4 (PD27, 2019-2), Blaney P30→10 (2019-2), Elliott P35→10 (PD25, 2023), Berry P35→12 (PD23, 2025), Erik Jones P34→11 (PD23, 2022). The deep-PD spots that hit belong to good cars that qualified poorly.
