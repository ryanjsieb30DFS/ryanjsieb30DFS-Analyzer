# North Wilkesboro Speedway

**UNVERIFIED — built from this slate's articles only** (DDD 7/19/26 + DFR flat-track data images). No logged autopsy at this venue yet; verify after the first points-race autopsy.

## Track profile
- 0.625-mile oval, 14° banking in the turns; the track climbs uphill on the backstretch and descends downhill on the frontstretch (DDD). One of the oldest tracks on the circuit.
- **First Cup points race here since 1996** (7/19/26 slate). All prior NextGen data is All-Star format (2023–2025, fields capped at 23 cars) — limited utility, especially for place differential (DDD note).
- Closest comparables per DDD: **Richmond** (0.75-mi, same 14° banking, wide corners) first, then Martinsville and New Hampshire (12°, true ovals), plus Phoenix, Gateway, Iowa, and the LA Coliseum / Bowman-Gray Clash races.
- 2023 All-Star ran on the old worn surface (lots of place differential paid); **2024–25 ran on the new repave** — track position racing, hard to pass the leader.

## DFS structure (from the 10 most recent comparable flat-track races, DFR)
- **Front-row dominator:** in all but 2 of 10 flat-track optimals, a front-row starter was the main dominator; polesitters led 292 / 304 / 208 / 274 laps in the big cases. At NW All-Stars the polesitter led 50+ laps in 2 of 3 (the third led 62 before a DNF).
- **Optimal-slot mix by start:** Top 5 = 30.0%, Top 10 = 36.7%, 11th–20th = 21.7%, 21st–30th = 25.0%, worse than 30th = 16.7%. Back-of-field starters average just 1.1 top-10 DK scores per race — PD upside is largely unrealized at this track type.
- All 7 NW/Clash short-flat optimals rostered **multiple top-5 starters**; 5 of 7 also had multiple double-digit-PD drivers.
- 7/19/26 race: **450 laps** — huge dominator pool; DDD expected a long green first stage (lap-down risk for sub-P30 starters).
