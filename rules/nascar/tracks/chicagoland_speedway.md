# Chicagoland Speedway — Joliet, IL

**VERIFIED — first NextGen race here autopsied 2026-07-12 (raced 2026-07-05).** First Cup race here since 2019. The pre-race profile below was extrapolated from comparable intermediates; the **Per-slate observations** section at the bottom carries what the 2026-07-05 race actually proved. Where they conflict, trust the observations.

## Track profile
- **1.52-mile tri-oval intermediate**, D-shaped configuration, **18° banking** in the turns.
- **Notable tire degradation / high tire wear** — the defining trait. Comparable tire-wear tracks: Darlington, Homestead. Comparable banking/shape: Kansas, Las Vegas (D-shaped intermediates).
- Multiple grooves exist (room to race side-by-side).
- **Treacherous surface**: patches and seams over bumps in the corners; a pit-road entry that "sneaks up" off turn 4 with a sharp exit before the commitment line → expect **pit-road penalties / missed stops** (a source of chaos + PD).
- Because of high tire wear, expect **comers-and-goers with fast laps spread throughout the field as drivers pit off-cycle** — raises upside for the whole field but makes fast-lap distribution unpredictable (article: AJ Allmendinger scored 29 fast laps at Darlington this year without leading a lap).

## Intermediate optimal-lineup profile (2026 season, from DFR reference tables)
Across the 6 intermediate optimals so far this season (Nashville, Charlotte, Texas, Kansas, Darlington, Las Vegas):
- **Every optimal except Texas rostered ≥3 drivers starting top 15.** More laps than lower series → more dominator points → front-runners with little PD upside still hit.
- **A front-two-rows starter (P4 or better) with double-digit laps led was in every posted optimal**, usually at $9,500+; all but Darlington featured multiple $10,000+ drivers → elite high-priced teams are strong here.
- **All 6 optimals rostered multiple JGR/23XI Toyotas** — clearly the best org on this track type in 2026.
- **Value (<$6,000) optimal appearances** started 11th, 22nd, 26th, 30th, 18th, 19th, 22nd, 15th, 30th → scores 30–53 fpts. **4 of 6 optimals featured 2 sub-$6,000 drivers** → punting is viable if the punt can sniff 30 points.
- Starting-position → Top-10 DK score distribution (DFR): Top 5 = 28.3%, Top 10 = 43.3%, 11–20 = 25.0%, 21–30 = 13.3%, worse-than-30th = 18.3%. Optimal-lineup share: Top 5 = 27.8%, Top 10 = 36.1%, 11–20 = 30.6%, 21–30 = 25.0%, worse-than-30th = 8.3%.
  - Read: **worse-than-30th is a THIN optimal bucket (~8%)** — a bit more top-10-DK than optimal comes from the very back, but those are usually $7–8k value, not the true punt path. **The 11th–30th band supplies ~55% of optimal spots — the mid-pack PD "bread and butter" tier.** Mirrors the Pocono/Sonoma mid-pack-PD lesson.

## Per-slate observations

### 2026-07-05 — eero 400 (first NextGen race here; autopsied 2026-07-12)
- **HMS did NOT "step off" — the article's "Toyota >> HMS in 2026" org read was wrong here.** William Byron (HMS) was the single best driver on the slate: **85.25 FPTS at 10-13% own**, in 40-80% of winning lineups; Alex Bowman (HMS) anchored the #1 lineup of the 23,461 field. The codified HMS-intermediate edge held; demoting it on one article's narrative forfeited the slate's biggest leverage dominator. → new lesson `narrative-suppressed-elite-is-leverage`.
- **Both orgs split hit/bust — dominator SELECTION, not org, decided the slate.** Toyota: Hamlin 70.8 and Briscoe 71.85 hit; Reddick **-10.8** (and HMS Larson **-12.5**) cratered from strong starts — the #1/#2 fish traps, 0% of winners. High tire wear + off-cycle chaos DID produce comers-and-goers: front-running favorites wrecked/faded while the mid-pack surged. Winning score 362.5 (high-ceiling slate).
- **Mid-pack (P11-30) PD was the meat, as predicted.** Cindric (P23) 45.9, Bubba Wallace 60.0, Daniel Suarez 41.5, plus Herbst (P18) and Gilliland (P29) in the top lineups — the 11-30 band supplied the value exactly per the DFR intermediate profile and the Pocono/Sonoma mid-pack-PD lesson.
- **Deep-back CHALK paid — not a trap.** Corey Heim (P28, 46-59% own) scored 55.1 and was in most winners; McDowell (P38, ~42% own) a milder 24.7. Consistent with Sonoma: don't fade deep-back chalk on base rate alone — add mid-pack PD alongside it, don't substitute.
- **Winning blueprint:** 1-2 dominators (Hamlin/Briscoe) + **Byron as the low-owned HMS leverage dom** + Bubba Wallace + Heim + a P11-30 PD stack (Cindric / Herbst / Gilliland). Winners ran avg own ~18-27 with 2-3 sub-10% darts; the user's portfolio was too chalky (avg own ~31, one single-entry lineup with 0 darts) — leverage_pct shark-gap -16.7. Chicagoland races like a high-tire-wear intermediate where the front-running chalk can crater and the P11-30 band + a narrative-suppressed elite carry the ceiling.
