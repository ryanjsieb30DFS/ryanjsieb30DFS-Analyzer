# Sonoma Raceway

**PARTIALLY VERIFIED — built from articles (2026-06-28, DFR/Dustin's Deep Dive); one NextGen race now observed live (2026-06-28 autopsy below). The multi-year start-distribution base rates still need a second source; the live race already qualified one of them (see deep-back note).**

1.99-mile road course, 10 turns, ~160-ft elevation change (high point Turn 3a, low point Turn 10). Technical, precision-demanding. One of NASCAR's two original road courses (with Watkins Glen); deep history to mine.

## DFS profile (NextGen era)

- **Dominator points are limited but concentrate on one car.** Each of the past several Sonoma optimal lineups rostered a driver who led **32+ laps**. 10 of the past 12 Cup road-course races here featured a driver starting **5th or better** in the optimal lineup. Unless a driver is severely underpriced or two split the laps evenly, more than one top-5 starter being optimal is rare.
- **A top-5 starter has been optimal in 4 of the past 5 Sonoma races.** Pole/front-row track position matters more here than at chaotic road courses.
- **Passing is hard → mid-pack solid finishes beat deep-back darts.** Over the past 5 years, **70% of top-10 DK scores came from drivers starting 20th or better.** Worse-than-30th is optimal only **6.7%** of the time at Sonoma — even thinner than the ~19.7% deep-back rate across road courses generally, and thinner than Pocono's 9.5%.
- **Starting-position optimal distribution (past 5 Sonoma races):** Top 5 = 13.3%, Top 10 = 23.3%, 11–20 = 30.0%, 21–30 = **40.0%**, worse-than-30th = **6.7%**. The **11th–30th band is ~70% of all optimal spots** — this is the place-differential meat. With the exception of 2024, each optimal lineup had **3–4 drivers starting 20th or better**.
- **Salary occasionally left on the table:** 2 of the NextGen Sonoma races left over $2,500 in salary — unusual outside superspeedways.
- **Format:** standard points race, 2 stages, no overlay. Stage-point-free drivers (those out of playoff contention / starting deep) can flip both stages and optimize the finish — a small edge for finishing upside (noted for Zilisch, McDowell, Hill).
