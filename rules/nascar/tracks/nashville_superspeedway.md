## Strategic notes

- **Track-position track / very hard to pass for the lead.** Position changes happen on **pit stops and restarts**, not green-flag passing. The best restarters (e.g. Chastain historically) over-perform their raw speed.
- **Low variance / low carnage.** Typical DNF count ~4 (2023: 2 DNFs; 2025: 3). The 2024 race with 13 DNFs is the outlier — do NOT treat back-marker PD as a repeatable mechanic here. Worse-than-30th starters are only ~6.7% of historical optimal-lineup spots.
- **Dominator points are spread, not concentrated.** When the front-runners run near-identical lap times, the lead (and fast laps) rotate through pit/restart cycles — so the dominator is hard to predict pre-race. **Multiple-dominator builds have been optimal in 4 of the last 5 Cup races here** (2025 Blaney+Hamlin; 2023 Chastain+Truex+Hamlin; 2022 Hamlin+Elliott; 2021 Larson mega-dom). Single-mega-dom (Larson '21, 264 laps led) is a rarer archetype.
- **Practice data correlates well** with race performance here (except the chaotic 2024).
- **The polesitter has posted strong DK scores every year** (fantasy-points ranks of 7th/1st/1st/10th/14th), but "strong DK score" ≠ optimal — a standard dominator line (~75 laps led, ~40 fast laps, P3 finish) scores only ~75 pts, which the cheap deep-PD plays can match.
- **Per-slate observation (5/31/26):** unusually deep, cheap place-differential pool (Chastain P35/$8.1k, Cindric P36/$6.9k, Zilisch P38/$6.4k) made 2-dominator builds easily affordable.
