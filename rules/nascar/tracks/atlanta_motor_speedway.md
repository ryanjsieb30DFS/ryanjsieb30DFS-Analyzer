# Atlanta Motor Speedway

**PARTIALLY VERIFIED — profile built from 2026-07-12 articles (DFR Deep Dive + graphics); the 7/12/26 running is now results-verified (autopsy 2026-07-14). See Per-slate observations below. One results data point; confirm trends at the next Atlanta autopsy.**

## Track profile

- **1.5-mile asphalt oval, but races as a SUPERSPEEDWAY** since the reconfiguration. Banking raised to 28° (from 24°) and the racing surface narrowed 15 feet → tight pack racing like Daytona/Talladega, not a traditional intermediate. **Do not treat as a normal 1.5-mile intermediate** — the HMS-aero-at-intermediates edge does not cleanly transfer here (drafting, not org downforce speed).
- **High caution rate:** avg **9.4 cautions / 57 laps** across the 7 Cup races on the new surface — significantly higher than old Atlanta. Chaos/variance reigns.
- **DNFs:** typically ~12-14; range observed 8-16. Plan on **10-12**.
- Shorter than Daytona → **more laps, more dominator points available** than a plate track, but the same premise holds: floor is lava for front-runners, ceiling lives at the back.

## Optimal-lineup starting-position distribution (DFR, 9 races on new surface)

| Start band | % of optimal spots |
|---|---|
| Top 5 | 11.1% |
| Top 10 | 24.1% (incl. dominators) |
| 11th–20th | 18.5% |
| **21st–30th** | **40.7% (the meat)** |
| Worse than 30th | 16.7% |

- **21-30 is the leverage meat** — matches the codified "mid-pack PD is the leverage meat" heuristic; at Atlanta the band skews slightly deeper (21-30 vs the intermediate 11-30).
- Worse-than-30th pays at **16.7%** — higher than Pocono (9.5%) / Sonoma (6.7%); deep-back PD is genuinely live here given the caution rate.
- 6 of 9 optimal lineups rostered 2 drivers starting 10th or better (dominators), but 8 of 9 also had 4+ drivers starting 15th or worse — **dominator + PD blend, not all-back.**

## Dominators / laps-led

- Laps led are **sporadic and spread** (Image 4). Pole-sitter has the easiest path to dom points but the **biggest downside in a wreck**. Last spring the top-3 lap leaders (started 2/3/4) finished 28/25/12 — dom points negated by carnage.
- Multi-year avg laps led (2024-26, Image 5): **Cindric 40.8, Logano 39.2, Larson 15.4, Blaney 14.2, Reddick 14.2, Ty Gibbs 13.8, Gilliland 13.0, Keselowski 11.6, Bubba Wallace 11.4, McDowell 11.4, Berry 11.2, Elliott 10.4.**
- **Blaney:** 8 top-10s in 9 races since 2022 (one last-place) — elite floor for a plate track.
- **Elliott** won here last year. **Keselowski** 2nd in 2025 and 2023.

## Superspeedway-specialist cheap cars to remember

Preece (9th spring), Zane Smith (7th), SVG (6th), Suarez (5th), McDowell, Berry (led 56 laps here) — random mid/back cars get elite finishes at Atlanta.

## Notes

- Toyota teams reportedly brought a **high-downforce setup** this race (hurts qualifying, helps in-race maneuverability late).
- **Salary can be non-binding**: with Bell/Byron/Hamlin/Reddick all expensive AND the front-dominators (Blaney/Logano) pricey, cheap PD cars matter more than usual.

## Per-slate observations

### 2026-07-12 — Quaker State 400 (first results-verified running; autopsy 2026-07-14)

- **Chalk dominators HIT — "floor is lava" was OVERSTATED this running.** The front-draft studs scored big: **Blaney 91.35** (slate high — confirms the "8 top-10s in 9 races" elite plate floor), **Bell 74.6, Reddick 71.8, Ty Gibbs 70.6, Hamlin 48.4.** The winning shape STACKED multiple doms; it did not fade off them.
- **Winning lineups were multi-dominator + moderately chalky, NOT contrarian.** #1 in both SE contests = **Reddick + Blaney + Bell + Ty Gibbs** + cheap filler (Zane Smith / Cole Custer / Noah Gragson), avg_own ~38%. Pack racing keeps the lead draft bunched, so several chalk doms finished together — they **correlated, not substituted**. - **Multi-year avg laps-led was a WEAK per-race dominator signal.** The Image-5 avg-LL leaders under-delivered (**Logano 30.2**, a field fish trap; **Cindric** moderate) while the day's fast cars (Blaney/Bell/Reddick/Gibbs) dominated. Weight current form / practice / quali pace over career avg-LL here. → [[nascar-2026-07-14-multiyear-lapsled-weak-perrace-signal]]
- **21-30 mid-pack PD CONFIRMED:** Gilliland (P21, 6% own) was in the #2 lineup of both contests. But the deep-back band (>30th, projected 16.7% of optimal) mostly did **not** pay this running — the min-salary carnage darts (McLeod/Finchum) missed.
- **Mid-owned dominators were the winning GLUE, not just sub-10% cars.** **Ty Gibbs (22% own, 70.6)** and **Carson Hocevar (18% own, 65.45)** were slate-definers in 35-45% of top lineups. Do NOT skip the 15-25%-owned dominator band at Atlanta — the strategy omitted both entirely.
