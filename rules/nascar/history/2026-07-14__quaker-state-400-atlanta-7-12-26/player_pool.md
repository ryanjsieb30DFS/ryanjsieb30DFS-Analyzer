1. **Ryan Blaney** ($10,200, 16% DF) — *Core* — The premier leverage dominator: DDD 3-star Top Dom, P1 pole, and "8 top-10s in 9 races since 2022 — absurd for this track type." Wins by leading laps from the front at ⅓ the ownership of the deep-back chalk doms; risk is the polesitter's lava floor in a 9.4-caution wreckfest.
2. **Joey Logano** ($9,300, 14% DF) — *Core* — P2, DDD Top Dom, 39.2 avg laps led (2nd on slate, Image 5) — the biggest laps-led equity on the board at 14% own. Wins by front-running a big dom line; risk is his own erratic finishes (DDD "way more erratic") plus front-row carnage.
3. **Christopher Bell** ($9,500, 45% DF) — *Core* — Top projection (57.35) and a DDD CORE "cash lock": deep-back dom+PD from P32. The modal anchor — wins on a 55+ dom line from the back; risk is it's the chalkiest name on the slate (Anchor-Equivalence: run Reddick/Byron as the alternative, don't stack all four).
4. **Tyler Reddick** ($10,500, 42% DF) — *Core* — 56.8 proj, P31, led 53 laps in the 2026 race here (Image 5) — the anchor-equivalent to Bell. Wins by dominating from the back; risk is chalk concentration and the same deep-start wreck exposure.
5. **Austin Cindric** ($8,200, 15% DF) — *Pivot* — DDD Top Dom and the slate leader in avg laps led (40.8, Image 5) from P8, at just 15% own. Wins by leading a pile of laps + a top-5; risk is a top-10 start that "must finish top 5" to be optimal (DDD).
6. **Kyle Larson** ($10,000, 8% DF) — *Pivot* — The suppressed elite: DDD 2-star, P3, 15.4 avg laps led, led 48 in the 2026 race, yet only 8% owned. Wins as a dom-leverage dart if the run stays caution-light; risk is a poor Atlanta finishing record (27.0 avg) — this is the drafting edge, not the HMS edge.
7. **Chase Elliott** ($9,900, 15% DF) — *Pivot* — DDD 3-star, P7, "won here last year." Underowned front-runner who wins with a top-3 + a few laps led; risk is a top-10 start that needs to finish top-10 (~50% odds per DDD) to avoid killing the lineup.
8. **William Byron** ($9,700, 41% DF) — *Core* — DDD CORE, deep-back dom from P26, with two Atlanta wins (Summer '23; Spring '22 led 111 laps, Image 2). The Anchor-Equivalence alternative to Bell/Reddick — run him as the anchor in place of, not alongside, the others; risk is chalk own with lower laps-led equity than the Penske front pair.
9. **John H. Nemechek** ($5,700, 9% DF) — *Pivot* — The meat-band PD play DDD spotlights: P24, "a 10th-place finish nets 48 points plus fast laps — very doable." Wins on a single top-10 from the 21–30 band (40.7% of optimal, Image 3) at sub-10% own; risk is the "bad vibe" quiet finish if he stalls mid-pack.
10. **Todd Gilliland** ($6,100, 6% DF) — *Pivot* — P21 at the top of the meat band, 13.0 avg laps led (Image 5, 7th on slate) — sleeper-spike floor at 6% own. Wins on a top-12 + a stage caution → 40+ PD; risk is the "people just don't like playing him" profile that also means a quiet mid-pack day.
