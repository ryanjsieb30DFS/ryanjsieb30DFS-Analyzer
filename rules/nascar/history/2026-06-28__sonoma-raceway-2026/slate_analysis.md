## How to approach the slate
Winning Sonoma builds need **one 32+-lap dominator** plus **3–4 drivers from the P11–30 PD band** — that band is ~70% of optimal spots, and deep-back darts (>P30) hit only 6.7% of the time, so do **not** punt to the back row chasing the 2024 Erik-Jones-from-P38 outlier. Anchor an elite (SVG **or** Gibbs/Larson/Briscoe), then differentiate downstream through the mid-pack value tier, where Sonoma's points actually come from.

Chalk-vs-contrarian: with no contest declared and recent fields at 25K+, lean **large-field contrarian** — the field is concentrated on SVG (66%) and on Wallace (33%) + Keselowski (24%) for PD. The edge is **pivoting the dominator down to Gibbs/Larson/Briscoe** and **sourcing PD from the lower-owned P11–30 names** (Preece, Suarez, Zane Smith, Custer, Gilliland, Cindric) instead of the two PD chalk magnets. Sharp-envelope target: ceiling over median, every lineup unique, a balanced ~mid-teens-to-20s ownership per slot (NASCAR runs chalkier than PGA — don't force PGA numbers), and **at least one genuinely low-owned PD piece** in most builds to fix the documented NASCAR leverage gap (19% vs your 59% in PGA). Two dominator paths minimum; never let a single sub-15% play be load-bearing.

## Decisions
1. **Anchor-Equivalence — MIX SVG with Gibbs (do NOT run SVG/Gibbs at parity).** SVG (66%) and Gibbs/Larson/McDowell/Briscoe are DDD-declared "interchangeable" dominators; at least one build MUST run the alternative (Gibbs, pole, 27%). Mechanism: chalk-tier dominators at far-apart ownership are mathematically substitutable to the field, so the alt captures uncontested leverage if he leads — but per the Pocono refinement, size the alternative for **leverage (1–2 lineups), not a 50/50 hedge** that caps your modal-world equity.
2. **PLAY mid-pack PD, PASS deep-back PD chalk.** Source place differential from the **P11–30 band** (Preece, Suarez, Zane Smith, Cindric, Custer, Gilliland) and fade **Keselowski (P35, 24%)** relative to field. Mechanism: Sonoma's deep-back bucket is 6.7% of optimal vs 70% for P11–30; the field's deep-back PD chalk is the lowest-probability path (Pocono trap, live again).
3. **Hold SVG to ≥33% but UNDER field rate — do not expand to 66%.** Mechanism: the 50% Chalk Rule forbids zeroing a structurally elite road-course chalk, but the Watkins-Glen "expand toward field rate" only applies when price/start/track **all three** confirm — here the start pillar fails (P6, no PD, never won outside top-3, Trackhouse off in practice), so bet-size to ~33–45%, not 66%. The under-ownership IS the leverage.
4. **MIX Elliott as a PD flier, not a core (the make-or-break call).** Mechanism: elite Sonoma track record (top-5 three straight) vs a genuine current-speed deficit (21st in 5-lap practice) — positive PD path from P18 at $8K, but the speed flag caps conviction; field rate or just below (DailyFan 26%), not an overweight.
5. **Route the Toyota road edge through Gibbs/Reddick/Briscoe, PASS Bell.** Mechanism: org-level Toyota speed is real, but Bell drives a precision road course with a fractured wrist (12% own for cause) — capture the equipment edge through the healthy Toyotas, per the codified Glen lesson.
