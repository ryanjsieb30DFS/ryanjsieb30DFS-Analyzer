# NASCAR — Player pool (Sonoma Raceway)
Fades are already removed; this is a ranked, GPP hand-build reference of single players only — no lineups, no pairings.

1. **Ty Gibbs** ($9,100, 27% own per DailyFan) — *Core* — The leverage anchor: pole-sitter, DDD's "GPP Fav," Toyota's road-course horsepower edge, and the math is forgiving (P4 + ~9 dom pts = 45, "all he needs"; ceiling is 20 fast laps / 50+ led). Wins by leading stage 1 wire-to-wire at 39 pts less ownership than SVG. Risk: could lose the lead lap 1 to Hocevar or finish outside the top 10 on bad strategy.
2. **Shane Van Gisbergen** ($14,000, 66% own per DailyFan) — *Core* — The slate's highest ceiling (68.5 proj, road king, led 97 at Sonoma '25); wins by simply leading 75 and cruising. But he starts P6, has never won outside the top 3, Trackhouse "looked a step off" in practice, and DDD is "strongly considering going underweight" — play him under his 66% field rate, not at it. Risk: $14K from P6 means his entire value is dom-dependent and the laps may go to Gibbs/Larson/McDowell.
3. **Chris Buescher** ($8,700, 29% own per DailyFan) — *Core* — DDD CORE: P15 road course = top-10 finish + place-differential, and he was top-4 in his first three NextGen Sonoma starts ("no clue why nobody played him last week"). Wins on a P8-ish finish (~43) with a few PD points. Risk: needs to actually convert the top-10 he's capable of — last year's 16th was the down outcome.
4. **Chase Briscoe** ($8,900, 16% own per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — DDD's "absolute favorite contrarian play": P7, top-3 finishing upside plus a few PD points and Toyota ammo, at half SVG's ownership — the cleanest dominator-adjacent leverage off Gibbs. Risk: "about as trustworthy as a rat" — he sometimes no-shows when you need him most.
5. **Connor Zilisch** ($10,000, 29% own per DailyFan) — *Core* — DDD CORE: qualified so badly (P17) he needs no dom — P8 alone ≈ 45, and as a non-points-chaser he can flip both stages for finishing upside (50+ ceiling). Wins on raw road-course pace once the green flag drops. Risk: the Trackhouse practice flag (19th in 5-lap avg) — the same speed worry that haunts SVG.
6. **Kyle Larson** ($9,500, 17% own per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — Real contrarian dominator equity: won here in 2024, ranked 1st in 10-lap practice average, starts P3. Wins by stealing the laps SVG is priced to lead, at a quarter of his ownership — the one HMS car that beats the road-course fade. Risk: DDD slots him "5th of that group" of dom candidates; conviction on the path, not the probability.
7. **Tyler Reddick** ($10,500, 22% own per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — Secondary dominator with a 50+ ceiling (3rd + 8 fast laps ≈ 53), affordable specifically in the non-SVG builds; 23XI Toyotas have been "monsters on road courses." Risk: DDD pegs him as more likely a 6th–8th-place guy — the ceiling needs the fast laps to show up, not just the finish.
8. **Michael McDowell** ($9,300, 18% own per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — P4 in a top-5 car, finished 2nd and 4th the last two Sonomas, and has no need to chase stage points — DDD: top-3 + 10 fast laps clears 45. Wins as the interchangeable dominator the field is lightest on. Risk: limited dom upside from P4 if he can't grab the lead; more finishing-PD than laps-led.
9. **Bubba Wallace** ($6,900, 33% own per DailyFan) — *Core* — The chalk mid-pack PD play in the right band: P26, 2nd last week on a road course, 16th = 37 pts at $6.9K. Wins by converting recent road form into a top-15 (~40). Risk: it's the crowded version of the play — qualifying-wreck car damage unknown, and 8 cheaper drivers in the same band can match him.
10. **Ryan Preece** ($7,000, 21% own per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — The lower-owned mirror of Wallace: P19, DDD likes him "a lot," floor ~32 and P8 ≈ 47 (which DDD calls optimal). Wins as the same mid-pack PD profile the field is underweighting. Risk: needs to crack the top 10 for the ceiling; a 15th is fine but not slate-defining.
11. **Daniel Suarez** ($7,500, 17% own per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — P21 in the PD sweet spot with the best floor of the value tier ("solid for all formats"); teammates qualified P2/P4 so there's speed in the car. Wins on a 12th-place finish (~40) with PD. Risk: ceiling is fringe top-10, not a dominator path — more floor than spike.
12. **AJ Allmendinger** ($8,200, 17% own per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — Posted the best 5-lap practice average on the slate and fights for top-5s here when it goes right (6th in '23 and '24); only needs ~41 (5th place ≈ 42). Wins if the practice speed turns into a top-5 from P8. Risk: tight window — anything worse than 8th from P8 is a bust, and the floor isn't safe.
13. **Ryan Blaney** ($8,500, 27% own per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — Posted the fastest single lap in practice (8th in 5-lap avg); a top-10 finish plus a few PD points gets him to the ~45 threshold. Risk: DDD is "worried he just fizzles out," and at 27% he's the priciest of the top-10 logjam without dom equity.
14. **Cole Custer** ($5,300, 8% own per DailyFan) — *Dart* — DDD-high sub-10% salary-saver: P27 gives PD equity and the salary the elite-anchor builds need; 18th ≈ 34 is "a lot more likely than anyone in the group below finishing top 20." Wins as a cheap scorer the field skips. Risk: could just as easily finish 29th and "make you look stupid."
15. **Todd Gilliland** ($6,100, 9% own per DailyFan) — *Dart* — Best floor of the sub-$6.5K group: 5 top-10s in 26 road starts (~18%), P29 so a 19th ≈ 34 at a saver price. Wins as the rare cheap driver who actually cracks the top 20. Risk: this has been a weak track for him (24th/24th/22nd outside his lone 2024 top-10); needs the start to do the work.
16. **Chase Elliott** ($8,000, 26% own per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — The make-or-break flier: elite Sonoma record (top-5 three straight, 2nd-best NextGen avg finish) and P18 PD upside at a cheap $8K — P8 ≈ 46. Wins if the track record beats the radar. Risk: a "legit lack of speed" (21st in 5-lap practice) — DDD's most conflicted call on the board; field rate, not an overweight.
17. **Zane Smith** ($6,600, 17% own per DailyFan) — *Dart* — P24 top-15 PD value and a clean salary saver in elite-anchor builds; 18th = 30 at a high rate, 13th = 41 as the spike (Suarez was optimal at this exact price in 2021). Risk: more 18th-for-30 than 13th-for-41 — a floor play wearing a ceiling's clothes.
18. **Austin Dillon** ($5,800, 18% own per DailyFan) — *Dart* — DDD's "favorite value play": P30 sits in Sonoma's 21st–30th optimal band (40% of optimal spots), RCR is strong on road-course strategy, and the floor beats the punt group below — DDD likes 35+ pts. Risk: not a super-reliable road racer; the upside is a good finish, not a dominator path.
19. **Austin Cindric** ($6,200, 17% own per DailyFan) — *Dart* — P23 in the PD band, a Penske car at a saver price; 15th = 36 is the path, and he's a lower-owned mirror of the Wallace tier. Risk: "dogshit lately" with recent Sonoma finishes of 25th/22nd/30th — a mix-at-random piece, not a conviction play.
20. **Riley Herbst** ($6,400, 15% own per DailyFan) — *Dart* — P25 PD value behind fast 23XI equipment; 17th = 34 "isn't awful on slates like this," and he carries fringe top-15 upside. Risk: mediocre road skills of his own — DDD rates Smith and Wallace much more likely to actually hit the top 15.
21. **William Byron** ($8,400, 16% own per DailyFan) — *Pivot* — Contrarian top-10 dart: P13 with PD potential if he reaches the top 10, and he "liked his car" in practice. Risk: HMS road-course fade is in force — lap times "didn't pop," best Sonoma finish is 8th, and from P13 even a 10th doesn't crack 40.
22. **Christopher Bell** ($7,700, 12% own per DailyFan) — *Dart* — Genuinely good Sonoma car (9th/9th/5th) and Toyota speed; on paper a top-10 at $7.7K = ~40. But he's racing a precision road course with a fractured wrist — the codified Glen lesson routes the Toyota edge to Gibbs/Reddick/Briscoe, not him. Risk: the injury caps how often he actually delivers the top-10 the car is capable of — the trap.
23. **Alex Bowman** ($6,500, 10% own per DailyFan) — *Dart* — Pure large-field randomness sprinkle: averages roughly a 15th here and a 15th would be a fine score at $6.5K. Risk: DDD's "no-flyzone" — "piss poor since returning from vertigo," the speed isn't there, and he's a milking-a-paycheck read right now.
24. **Ricky Stenhouse Jr** ($5,500, 11% own per DailyFan) — *Dart* — The lone deep-back punt left in the pool (P33); only wins through "complete randomness" — a 20th on the right strategy ≈ 35. Risk: Sonoma's worse-than-30th bucket is just 6.7% of optimal and the track never produces the carnage this play needs; lowest-probability path on the board.
