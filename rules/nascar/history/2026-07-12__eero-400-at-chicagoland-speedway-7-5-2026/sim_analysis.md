The strategy names it the mandatory Anchor-Equivalence decision (Bell 24% / proj 58.0 > Reddick 39% / proj 57.1); the sim independently produces the exact same shape — Bell +12.1 (its top edge) while Reddick sits 10 pts under field — so the two are fully aligned on the leverage dominator, and on Hamlin as the anchor. Minor divergence the other way: the sim shows small positive leans on **Chastain (+3.7)** and **AJ Allmendinger (+3.3)** that the strategy explicitly PASSES/FADES on floor grounds — treat those as MME-only variance bullets, not a reason to overrule the strategy's single-entry fade.
