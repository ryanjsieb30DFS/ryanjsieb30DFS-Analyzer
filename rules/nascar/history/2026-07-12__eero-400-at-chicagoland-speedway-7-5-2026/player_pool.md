# NASCAR — Player pool

_Chicagoland Speedway (2026-07-05). Fades are already removed (AJ Allmendinger, Alex Bowman, Cody Ware, Connor Zilisch excluded); this is a ranked, GPP-framed hand-build reference of individual players — never a lineup._

**1. Denny Hamlin** ($11,000, 55% own — DailyFan; DDD ~50%, could balloon high-50s) — *Core* — The #1 dominator on the slate from P1, "unstoppable on this track type" per DDD, tested here in April. Wins on laps-led + fast-lap volume; the one chalk you ride, not fight — the only risk is that at 55% he's the field's floor, so he anchors rather than differentiates.

**2. Christopher Bell** ($10,000, 24% own — DailyFan, 58.0 proj = highest on the board after Hamlin) — *Core* — The leverage dominator and the slate's central edge: DDD's #2 dom ("one of my top plays," GPP Fav) and the vendor's own projection both rank him ABOVE Reddick, yet the field owns Reddick 15pts more. Wins via a 2nd-dom performance (2nd at Nashville & Charlotte, 61/44 fast laps) at a fraction of the ownership; risk is the bad-luck streak (Texas wreck) continuing.

**3. Erik Jones** ($7,200, 24% own — DailyFan) — *Core* — The mid-pack PD engine DDD raves about ("so good" on this track type — 13th/11th/2nd/6th at Charlotte/Nashville/Michigan/Pocono) starting P22. Only needs ~13th to clear 40 pts, so the ceiling path is a clean top-15 from the back at moderate ownership; risk is one of the ~20 top-15 hopefuls boxing him out at 18th–20th.

**4. Chase Briscoe** ($9,900, 14% own — DailyFan, 46.85 proj) — *Pivot* — Under-owned JGR race-winning dominator from P7; the $9.9k tag forces top-dom production, but that's exactly why he's cheap in ownership. Wins as a leverage pivot off the Hamlin/Bell chalk capturing the same Toyota long-run edge at 14%; risk is he's "a step off the top guys" and merely runs 5th with limited dom points.

**5. Kyle Larson** ($10,500, 30% own — DailyFan) — *Pivot* — Secondary dominator from P2 with April test laps and top-5 equity on every intermediate; a great starting spot, restarter, and clean-air fast-lap collector. Wins if he beats Toyota long-run speed to a huge score — but DDD's open question ("can he beat JGR after 15 laps?") is the exact ceiling cap, so he's a path, not the path.

**6. Corey Heim** ($7,500, 45% own — DailyFan) — *Core* — DDD "No brainer" CORE deep-back PD from P28: qualified early, moves forward, "struggling to envision a world where Heim isn't scoring at least 40." The ceiling is a top-15 charge with floor to match; the only knock is 45% ownership makes him the play everyone has, so he's a floor anchor, not leverage.

**7. Austin Cindric** ($7,000, 27% own — DailyFan) — *Pivot* — Mid-pack PD from P23 with 2nd-best 10-lap practice average and enough results (5th Darlington, 12th Kansas, 11th Michigan) to hit ~40 from a 13th. Same interchangeable ~40-pt ceiling as the rest of the band; risk is he's "a question mark" like Logano and lands 19th.

**8. Ryan Preece** ($6,700, 17% own — DailyFan, 37.0 proj) — *Pivot* — Better racer than qualifier; the RFK read (BK/Buescher qualifying top-5) implies all three RFK cars have legit pace, so Preece has hidden speed from P20. A 13th = ~37 pts and DDD argues 10th–11th is plausible; risk is deep-field traffic keeps him at his P20 start.

**9. Zane Smith** ($6,600, 5% own — DailyFan, 27.0 proj) — *Dart* — The sharpest sub-5% leverage shot: PROVEN intermediate top-10 speed this year on merit (9th Nashville, 10th Charlotte, 14th Vegas) from a mid-pack P16. Wins by delivering the same top-15 the 15%+ crowd needs at a third the ownership; risk is you need a near-perfect race for the ceiling to land.

**10. Riley Herbst** ($6,500, 4% own — DailyFan, 29.0 proj) — *Dart* — The cleanest sub-5% dart: 23XI equipment (the best org on the track type) from P18 with real results (11th Texas, 14th Kansas). Wins as a top-15 PD driver nobody has; DDD wants only "a sprinkling," and floor is thin, so this is a ceiling/leverage bullet, not a foundation.

**11. Tyler Reddick** ($10,700, 39% own — DailyFan, 57.1 proj) — *Pivot* — Genuine top-5 dominator with race-winning upside ("king of practice"), but the whole slate edge is UNDERWEIGHTING him: both DDD and the vendor's own Bell projection say he's the less-likely of the equivalent Toyota doms, and at 39% his lead is often "handed to him" without the fastest car. Play him light and take Bell instead; his ceiling is real but capped if Hamlin eats the available laps-led.

**12. Daniel Suarez** ($7,700, 19% own — DailyFan) — *Pivot* — Spire has been a consistent top-15 intermediate contender (won Charlotte as "a solid 8th place guy"); from P26 a top-15 clears 40 at $7.7k. A pivot off chalk Heim in the same PD band; risk is the "10 other guys who can do this" fear — cracks top-15 or he's a 20th-place dud.

**13. John H. Nemechek** ($5,700, 5% own — DailyFan, 24.0 proj) — *Dart* — Deep P17 PD at sub-5%: smashed Pocono (4th), 14th Michigan, and teammate Jones has been stellar on the track type, so the team speed is there for a top-15. DDD says large-field only ("shy away if you play 20 or fewer") — a ceiling/leverage dart whose floor (21st) is ugly.

**14. Josh Berry** ($5,500, 16% own — DailyFan, 28.0 proj) — *Dart* — DDD's favorite value play: price + upside, "can finish top-25 at a decent rate and get close to 30 pts," more likely than most in the range to reach it from P34. The salary-saver that frees room for two dominators; risk is going a lap down early on a long green run and scoring horribly.

**15. Shane Van Gisbergen** ($6,800, 18% own — DailyFan, 34.35 proj) — *Pivot* — Elite PD equity + strong tire management from P30, with back-to-front results on this track type (Darlington 33→14, Texas 30→17, plus 11th Charlotte / 5th Nashville). A P17 nets 39 — same equity as Jones/Preece who need 12th–13th; risk is Trackhouse being "dogshit" and SVG's small good-sample.

**16. Ricky Stenhouse Jr** ($6,200, 7% own — DailyFan) — *Dart* — "Super contrarian" per DDD, and that's the point: P24 sits dead-center in the P11–30 PD band that paid at Pocono (34.45) and Sonoma (33.0), and the sharp playbook names him a value scorer this account habitually skips. Sub-10% leverage; risk is he needs a top-15 that "even feels unlikely," so it's a ceiling bet.

**17. Todd Gilliland** ($5,300, 6% own — DailyFan) — *Dart* — Cheap sub-10% PD salary saver from P29 — the exact mid-pack-band start where Gilliland scored 35.5 at Pocono. Frees salary for two dominators while offering "occasional top-20 upside"; risk is these bottom guys need to gain track position at the right time or they run at the back.

**18. Michael McDowell** ($6,100, 24% own — DailyFan) — *Pivot* — Pass-through-penalty PD play from P38: floor ~20–25, average race ~30, and upside if he catches an early caution to stay on the lead lap and rips fast laps in clean air. The catch — DDD likes these BECAUSE "ownership is typically limited," but at 24% the leverage premise is already priced out; he's chalk, not the hidden angle.

**19. Brad Keselowski** ($8,000, 16% own — DailyFan) — *Pivot* — Contrarian dominator from P4 at a friendly $8k: "what if he runs top-6 all race and gets 18 fast laps for 50 pts?" (dominant 2nd at Darlington, 142 laps led). Wins as a cheap front-row fast-lap source; risk is the floor — an 8th-or-worse from P4 "is just really bad," and DDD doubts he leads much vs the Toyotas.

**20. Chris Buescher** ($8,700, 13% own — DailyFan) — *Pivot* — Same RFK contrarian-dom thesis from P3, harder to justify at $8.7k: solid when he starts up front (5th Texas from P3, led 41 at Darlington). The two RFK cars top-5 together "stands out too much" to ignore; risk is identical to BK's — a top-8-or-worse finish at this price is a dud, and long-run pace may trail Toyota.

**21. Bubba Wallace** ($8,500, 16% own — DailyFan, 38.7 proj) — *Pivot* — Contrarian intermediate finisher (3rd Michigan, 5th Kansas, 9th Vegas/Texas) with a few fast laps in his game from P8. You're paying for a theoretical top-5; DDD flags it "low % play" — a 7th with 8 fast laps is only ~42 at $8.5k, so the floor and the tight top-10-13 range cap it.

**22. Joey Logano** ($8,200, 26% own — DailyFan, 40.0 proj) — *Pivot* — Deep PD from P31 who "doesn't need an amazing finish" — 15th + 2 fast laps = 45, and he's won a slate from 33rd (Charlotte) with 14th Nashville / 7th Michigan on record. A 50/50 boom-or-bust: the speed has been "slow in practice, slow in qualifying," so the same P31 that gives upside can bury him at 24th.

**23. William Byron** ($9,700, 18% own — DailyFan) — *Pivot* — Contrarian HMS dominator from P10 — the easiest optimal path is a top-3 + 20 fast laps (~57 DK), plausible given flashes of top-5 speed. A leverage double-up angle for MME, but DDD is explicit Toyota > HMS on this track type in 2026, so this needs the org read to flip; a 10–15% MME piece, not a core.

**24. Chase Elliott** ($9,500, 15% own — DailyFan) — *Pivot* — Same HMS contrarian-dom profile from P9 (won Texas from P14 with 87 laps led, so the ceiling exists). Wins only on a top-3 + big fast-lap haul; DDD trusts JGR/Reddick over HMS here and calls Elliott/Byron "10–15% MME plays at most" — leverage upside gated behind an org read the article distrusts.

**25. Ross Chastain** ($7,800, 8% own — DailyFan, 30.0 proj) — *Dart* — Sub-10% MME variance bullet only: needs a top-10 he "hasn't managed all year" with Trackhouse "fighting for its life," and even a 13th is just 36 pts.
